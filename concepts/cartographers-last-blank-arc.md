# The Cartographer's Last Blank — Story Arc

> Save the Cat! beat sheet for the collaborative story.

---

## Theme

*"The most important thing on any map is the blank space you haven't filled yet."*

Sub-theme: You cannot map yourself by looking inward. You map yourself through the space between you and someone else.

---

## Characters

### Protagonist — Elara
- Age: mid-30s to early 40s
- Former apprentice to a master cartographer who believed everything could be charted with enough precision
- Broke away, built her own tower, spent a decade mapping intangible things (lost jokes, half-remembered dreams, roads not taken)
- Gift: seeing what others overlook
- Flaw: believes she can only understand things by keeping them at arm's length — by *drawing* them instead of *living* them
- Arc: from isolation → connection. From observer → participant.

### B Story — Kael
- Age: late 20s to early 30s
- A salt-rover: walks the long white shores trading news for salt and salt for stories
- Has a home somewhere south they never talk about
- Gift: patience, curiosity, the ability to sit beside someone without demanding answers
- Flaw: avoids root — the road is safer than staying. Follows rumors but never arrives.
- Arc: from wandering → choosing. From following threads → making their own map.

### Antagonist — Solenne Vath
- Age: late 50s
- Elara's former master. A cartographer of the old school — everything can be mapped, *must* be mapped, down to the last grain of sand.
- Runs the Cartographer's Guild in the capital city of Ashenport
- Believes blank spaces are failures, not invitations
- Has been searching for Elara since she left the tower — not out of care, but because an unmapped cartographer is a professional insult
- Gift: precision, authority, institutional power
- Flaw: cannot tolerate ambiguity. A blank space to her is a wound.
- She doesn't see herself as a villain. She thinks she's saving cartography from sentimentality.

### Supporting — Sera (net-mender in Low Tide)
- Point of connection. Knows more than she says. May reappear.

### Supporting — The top room (not a person, but functions as one)
- The mystery of what's at the top of the tower drives Elara's arc
- May or may not contain someone/something

## Save the Cat! Beats

### 1. Opening Image (0-1%)
Elara alone in her tower, surrounded by maps of intangible things. She finishes a map — the map of swallowed apologies — and sets down her pen. She looks at her hands. She has no map of herself. A stranger's voice: "I'm looking for the cartographer." First knock at her door in years.

### 2. Theme Stated (5%)
Kael arrives at the tower. They show Elara the scrap of vellum they found in the hollow tree: *"Here be the one who draws."* Kael says: "Why is the center of your own map blank?" Elara has no answer. **Theme:** The blank space matters.

### 3. Set-Up (1-10%)
We see Elara's world: the tower, her routine, her avoidance. Flashbacks hint at Solenne Vath — the master who taught her that a cartographer's worth is measured by what they've finished, not what they've left open. Elara left the guild a decade ago with a single unfinished map of the tower's top room. She's been avoiding finishing it ever since.

### 4. Catalyst (10%)
Kael leaves. Elara watches them go. The tower feels smaller now. She unrolls the map of blank spaces and sees a new blank she hadn't noticed before: the shape of the conversation she just had. She can't map it. She has to go after it.

### 5. Debate (10-20%)
Elara paces. She packs. She unpacks. The tower is safe. The world is full of things she's only drawn, never touched. She looks at the map of the tower — the one where the top room has a door, not a window. She still hasn't climbed it. She leaves the map on the desk, takes nothing but her pens and a blank book, and walks out.

### 6. Break into Two (20%)
Elara leaves the tower for the first time in years. She doesn't know where she's going. She only knows she's looking for Kael — or rather, looking for the person she became in that conversation. **She enters a new world: the world she's only ever drawn.**

### 7. B Story (22%)
She finds Kael in Low Tide, at the crooked pier. Their reunion is quiet — no grand gestures, just two people happy to see each other. The B story begins: the space between them becomes the real map.

### 8. Fun and Games (20-55%)
The journey. Traveling north together. Elara draws everything she used to draw from memory, now from life. She discovers the difference between drawing a mountain and standing at its foot. She maps the space between herself and Kael — cross-hatching conversations, silences, the shape of shared laughter. Each day is a small revelation. She learns about salt-roving: Kael trades news for salt, salt for stories, never keeps either. She asks why. Kael deflects.

### 9. Midpoint (55%) — False Victory
They reach the ridge. Kael says: "This is where I turn. My road goes south." Elara realizes she's been drawing the space between them because she was afraid of what would happen when Kael left. She gives Kael the map of blank spaces with a new margin. Kael leaves. Elara is alone on the ridge — but this time, she doesn't turn back. She walks forward into the valley. **She believes she's learned enough. She hasn't.**

### 10. Bad Guys Close In (55-75%)
Elara reaches a town at the valley's heart. Solenne Vath's guild has agents everywhere — they've been tracking her since she left the tower. Elara is recognized. Solenne sends word: *Come back to the guild. Finish what you started. Your blank maps embarrass the profession.* Elara refuses. The guild freezes her access to ink and paper suppliers. She's trapped — she can't draw without materials, and she can't leave without being followed.

### 11. All Is Lost (75%)
Elara is cornered in an inn. A guild agent confiscates her blank book — the only thing she took from the tower. She has nothing. No maps, no pens, no Kael. Solenne arrives in person. "You were my best student. And you wasted your gift on ghosts and gaps." Elara has no answer, because part of her believes it's true. **The blank space she thought was an invitation now feels like a void.**

### 12. Dark Night of the Soul (75-85%)
Elara sits in a borrowed room, empty-handed. She tries to draw from memory — the tower, the cove, the ridge, Kael — but without ink or paper, she has nothing. She realizes she's spent her whole life believing that to understand something she had to *capture* it. But Kael was never captured. The conversation on the ridge was never captured. They just *happened*. And they mattered more than every map she'd ever made.

### 13. Break into Three (85%)
A knock at the door. Not Solenne. Kael.

They'd heard the rumor in a port three towns over — the cartographer was trapped. They came back. Not to save her. To sit beside her.

Kael doesn't say "I told you so." They say: "Draw me a map of this room. Not with ink. With words. Tell me what you see."

Elara closes her eyes and describes the room — the grain of the wood, the light through the shutters, the dust in the air, the sound of Kael breathing.

And she realizes: **the map was never the paper. The map was always the seeing.**

### 14. Finale (85-99%)
Elara and Kael return to the tower together. Not to hide — to finish.

Elara climbs to the top room for the first time. It's empty, except for a single window facing east, and a desk with a blank sheet of vellum that's been waiting a decade.

She sits. She picks up a pen. She doesn't draw the tower, or the view, or Kael, or herself.

She draws the space between where she was and where she is now. All of it. The tower, the cove, the drowned village, the ridge, the valley, the room, the inn, the moment she thought she'd lost everything, the moment she realized she hadn't.

She draws Kael's shadow at noon. She draws the splash of a child's shoe half-buried in mud. She draws the exact shape of a word hanging in the air.

And in the center, she writes: *"Here be the one who draws."*

Not as a confession. As a **decision.**

### 15. Final Image (99-100%)
Kael is walking south again — but this time they have a map in their coat. A map of the tower, drawn from the inside out, with a trail marked through every room, leading to the top. A way back.

And Elara is at the top of the tower, not drawing. Just watching the sun rise through the east window. No map between her and the world.

The last line of the story, if there is one: *She is no longer the cartographer. She is the one who sees.*
