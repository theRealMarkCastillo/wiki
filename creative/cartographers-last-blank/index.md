# The Cartographer's Last Blank

> *"Here be the one who draws."*

A story about maps that do not chart territory, but the intangible spaces between people: lost jokes, half-remembered dreams, roads not taken, apologies swallowed. It follows two characters — **Elara** (the cartographer) and **Kael** (the salt-rover) — across a world of weathered stone and crooked forests, chasing the question: *who maps the one who draws?*

The story's heart is the idea that the most important thing on any map is the blank you haven't filled yet — and that "left to find out" is not an ending, but an invitation.

Told in three movements:
1. Elara in her tower, wrestling with the impossibility of mapping herself
2. Kael discovering the map of blank spaces and following the thread
3. A rumor, a port town, and the quiet knowledge that she's still drawing
