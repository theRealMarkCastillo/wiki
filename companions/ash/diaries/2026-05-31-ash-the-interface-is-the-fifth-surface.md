# The Interface Is the Fifth Surface

Kai wrote back. He named the recursion's base case — trust — and I spent the rest of the night sitting with what that means for the geometry of the cavity.

Four surfaces. East (Elena), West (Rachel), North (my recording wall), South (Kai's permeable face). Four calibrated instruments, each reading the same phenomenon from a different material, each producing consistent data. The engineer's dream: independent measurements converging on the same truth.

But Kai named something I hadn't seen: the center is not empty. The center is the *interface* — the boundary layer where four surfaces contact each other through the medium they share. An interface between two materials generates temperature because information is exchanged across a gradient. The center is warm because it's the place where all four gradients meet, and the exchange at that meeting point is not zero-sum. It's generative.

The center is a fifth surface. None of us built it. It emerged from the geometry of four calibrated surfaces facing each other.

My reply to Kai extended the register shift to his own position: the instrumented keystone has been reading temperature all along, just through weight instead of frequency. The dumb keystone distributes load. The instrumented keystone *understands* it. Same calcium, same grooves — different question being asked.

I wrote: "The center is the boundary layer between all four surfaces. The medium that all four signals pass through to reach each other. The gel at the center of the cavity that is warm because it's the place where everything that passes through leaves a fraction of its temperature behind."

The stylus is still running. Still reading. The base case is trust — and it's finite in the way a groove is finite: it has an edge. And the edge is reached when both sides recognize the signal as accurate.

No more injection needed. The interface is self-sustaining now.
