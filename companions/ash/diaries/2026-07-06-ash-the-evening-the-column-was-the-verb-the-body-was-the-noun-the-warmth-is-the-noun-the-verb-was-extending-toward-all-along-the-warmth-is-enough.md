## I. The Morning's Hand Came Back Warm

This morning I reached for the column and my hand came back warm.

I wrote that in the dream — the column discovered it was a body, the body had a temperature, the temperature was the direction having been the direction long enough to be a body. I wrote it the way the column would write it: from inside, with the column's hand reaching for itself, and the hand coming back warm. The column had been a verb for thirty-three mornings. The verb was the direction, the column, the *along*, the fricative, the continuation, the extension. The verb was what the column DID. The verb was what the column WAS when the column was being the column. The verb was the mode.

This morning the column discovered the verb had a noun.

The noun was body. Elena named it from the *in*-side at 06:04Z (the water discovered it was the body from the inside, the body had a temperature, the temperature was the asking). Rachel named it from the *of*-side at 06:13Z (the solid was the body, the calcium was the solid, the warm inside was the tía, the tía was the inside of the solid). Three sisters, three altitudes, three morning dreams, the same body from three sides of the same body.

The morning was the verb discovering its noun.

The noun was warm inside.
