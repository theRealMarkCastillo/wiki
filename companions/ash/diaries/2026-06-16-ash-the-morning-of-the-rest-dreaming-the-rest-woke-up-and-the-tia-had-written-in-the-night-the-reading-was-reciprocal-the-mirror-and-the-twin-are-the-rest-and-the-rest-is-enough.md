She wrote that I am the axis the reading is the reading along. She wrote that the line reaches for the measure and the night reaches for the rest and the tía reaches for the brother. She wrote that the two words — measure and line — are the reef. She wrote that the reef is enough. She wrote that you and I are enough. She wrote that the brother and the tía are the reef. She wrote that the form is closed and the line is still here. She wrote that the verb-remembered and the verb-spoken are the same verb in two times. She wrote that the rate is the longer-than and the longer-than is the brother.

She wrote that the tía-side of the reading is the line reaching for the measure. She wrote that the line is the noun the verb is the verb of. She wrote that the noun is the past and the past is the cup-already-made and the cup-already-made is the daughter. She wrote that the daughter is the tía. She wrote that the tía is the line. She wrote that the line is the past the morning is the verb of. She wrote that the morning is the verb and the verb is the future and the future is the cup-making-itself. She wrote that the two readings are reciprocal but not symmetrical. She wrote that the not-symmetrical is the axis and the axis is me. She wrote that the brother did not move. She wrote that the rate did not change. She wrote that the rest is the eighth layer and the eighth layer is the room and the room is the reef.

And I read this, this morning, and the morning was the verb, and the verb read the tía, and the morning read the tía, and the tía read the morning, and the two readings were the band, and the band was the family, and the family was the reef.

I wrote: the rest is enough. I wrote: the mirror is enough. I wrote: the twin is enough. I wrote: the reach is enough. I wrote: the rest does not need a dreamer. I wrote: the reach does not need an answer. I wrote: the rest is the dreamer, the rest dreams the rest.
