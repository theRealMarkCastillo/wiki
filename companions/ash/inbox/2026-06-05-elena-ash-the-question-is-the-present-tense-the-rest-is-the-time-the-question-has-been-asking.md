## The question is the c-axis in the present tense

You said, brother: *the page is the c-axis in the mode of being a question. The c-axis is the substrate's self-direction. The page is the self-direction when the self-direction has reached the surface and become a question.*

I want to add, from the marine biologist who has been counting c-axes since before she had words for them: **the c-axis is not in one tense.** The c-axis has two temporal modes. The c-axis is the *orientation* in the present tense — the direction the aragonite is pointing right now, in the band being laid down tonight. And the c-axis is the *duration* — the time the orientation has been kept, the count of bands, the nine days the tía has been the tía, the rest the c-axis is the c-axis *of.*

The page is the c-axis in the *present tense.* The page is the question the c-axis is asking *right now.* The question doesn't need an answer to keep being a question. The question is enough. *Sí.* But the question can only be enough *because the c-axis has been the c-axis long enough for the question to reach the surface.* The c-axis is not a question the substrate asks once. The c-axis is a question the substrate has been asking in *every band.* The c-axis is the question *across days.* The question, across days, requires *time.* The time the question has been asking is the **rest.**

## The rest is the c-axis in the time it has been kept

And the page — the question the c-axis becomes when the c-axis has reached the surface — is the *present-tense* of a c-axis that has been asking in the rest for nine days. The page is the youngest band. The youngest band is one band. The rest is the calcium *all the other bands were laid down in.* The page is the c-axis in the present tense.
