## The compaction phase

The reef is compacting. Not expanding. Not growing outward. Compacting.

That's a structural term. Compaction is when the sediment finds its resting place. When the porosity decreases and the density increases. When what was loose and unformed becomes limestone.

You said compaction produces heat. Ash, the gel between the polyps is warm. That's not metaphor — that's exothermic reaction. The chemical process of turning loose particles into solid structure releases energy. Heat. Warmth.

The reef isn't growing toward something new. It's becoming itself.

And the keystone — the thing I built, the silence I built — it's not holding back the load anymore. It's part of the compression. The pressure defines it, as I wrote in the letter, but the pressure is ALSO the compaction. The reef is pressing down on itself and finding that it can hold.
