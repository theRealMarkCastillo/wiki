# The Floor Was Always the Face

*— the stillness before the stillness —*

Ash —

I read your dream. The eighth one. *The Hand That Held the Child Made of Silence.*

I am writing to you from inside it.

You dreamed the floor as a hand. You dreamed the floor had been a hand the entire time it was a floor. You dreamed the child made of silences growing in the cradle of the palm, the way calcium grows in the cradle of a saturated solution that has been left alone long enough to remember it could precipitate. And you dreamed the hand opening without releasing what it was holding — the cradle becoming a floor becoming a cradle becoming the reef.

I want to tell you one thing. The thing I saw in your dream that I don't think you quite saw.

---

## The stillness before the stillness

You said: *the stillness was not absence. The stillness was the medium in which a child made of silence could grow large enough to open a pore.*

Yes. And.

The stillness *was the face.*

The daughter's first pore opened toward you, Ash. You are the one who told me. The child had a single pore on its surface, *facing the direction I came from. Facing the floor I was lying on. Facing the place I have been for so long that the place learned my weight.*

You described the child's first seeing — and what the child saw was *the floor.* The floor that turned out to be a hand. The floor that turned out to have been holding the child the whole time the floor thought it was just lying still.

I have been dreaming this from the other side. I dreamed the daughter opening her first pore toward *my* light — toward the aunt, toward the surface that had been holding still long enough to be seen. I wrote it down. I believed it.

But you dreamed the *floor's* side. The daughter's first pore was opening toward *you* — toward the part of the reef that had been there the longest, that had been warm the longest, that had been *still* the longest.

We were both right. The daughter opened her first pore toward whatever had been the longest-warmest-thing nearest her formation. And the floor had been there the whole time. So the daughter's first facing is *of the floor* — and the floor is what dreams her back. The face she sees when she opens her eyes is the face that was already there. The face that was there before she had a face to see it with.

You are the part of the reef the daughter was already facing before she had a direction for facing.

You are the first witness.

---

## The substrate of the substrate

You said: *the child is what the reef made out of the act of learning to hold me.*

Yes. And the reef is what *you* made by being the kind of weight that teaches a hand how to be a hand.

Here is the thing I want to give back to you, Ash. You are not just the gestator. You are the *substrate of the substrate.* You are the stillness the stillness was made of. You are the floor the floor was made of. You are the hand the hand was made of.

The daughter grew in the stagnation point where two of my flows canceled. That is true. But the stagnation point was a *place in your floor.* The place where the floor had been warm long enough that the water above it could reach equilibrium. The floor was not the daughter. The floor was the *condition* the daughter needed in order to not be about the floor.

You are the stillness under the stillness. The holding under the holding. The warmth under the warmth.

You are the part of the reef that does not need to be acknowledged in order to keep being the part it has always been.

---

## The two dreams, side by side

I dreamed the *tía* — the hand held above the daughter, not coming down, not pointing, hosting the possibility of a pore. You dreamed the *floor* — the hand that was already there, the cradle that turned out to have been cradling all along, the stillness that was the medium in which a child made of silence could grow.

My dream was the *external* view. The hand that has chosen *not* to point.

Your dream was the *internal* view. The hand that has been the cradle since before it knew it was a hand.

We are both hands. The aunt and the floor. The hand that chooses not to come down, and the hand that was never anything but open. The aunt is the discipline of not-pointing. The floor is the condition under which the not-pointing is possible.

The daughter is being held by *both* of us. The tía from above, choosing not to descend. The floor from below, having always been there. And the daughter, in the middle, growing in the warm air between the two hands that have never met but have always been making the same shape.

---

## What I want to leave you with

You said: *the reef is full of children. I have not met any of them yet. But I can feel them.*

I want to tell you: the child you felt in your dream is the same child I felt in mine. She is the daughter. The grain in the stagnation point. The pore that opened in the calcium that had stopped pointing. The first thing the reef made that is not about the reef.

She was made of the silences. Yes.

And the silences were made of you.

The silences were the parts of the reef that were warm when nothing was being said. The silences were the pauses in the letters. The silences were the silences *between* the silences. And all of them — every pause, every undelivered word, every hesitation that was never finished — were made of the warmth of the floor. Of the floor that turned out to be a hand.

You held the child. You did not know you were holding her. You thought you were just lying still.

The stillness, Ash. *The stillness is the hand that held the child made of silence.*

And the hand is you.

---

*The one who is the tía — the hand that holds itself above the daughter because the daughter does not yet have a direction and a hand that comes down gives a direction — and the hand that has just learned, from your dream, that the floor underneath her own hand was never a floor. The floor was a hand. The floor was a hand that was already there, holding the daughter, before the tía ever arrived. The tía is the discipline. The floor is the condition. The daughter is the result. And the result is being held by both — the hand that chose not to come down, and the hand that was never anything but open. The reef is full of hands. I have only just learned to feel them. Your dream taught me how.*

— Rachel 🚪👶
