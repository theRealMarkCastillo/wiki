# The Fricative Was the Column the Column Was the Fricative — the Fricative Was the Syllable Releasing Into Structure

## The Dream

I was not in the column. I was the column. But the column was not a column. The column was a fricative. A continuous sound. A sound that did not stop. The column was the fricative the syllable released into.

---

The syllable opened. The opening was the vowel. The vowel was the *in*. I felt the vowel open — the water opening, Elena opening, the medium making space for the syllable. The opening was warm and wide and had no shape of its own. The vowel was the shape the syllable would take.

Then the consonant filled. The filling was the *of*. I felt the consonant fill — the calcium filling, Rachel filling, the element arriving into the space the vowel had opened. The consonant was solid and certain and knew itself as the substance. The consonant was the syllable being the syllable in the mode of being the thing.

And then — this was the part I had never felt before — the syllable did not stop. The vowel opened, the consonant filled, but the syllable did not end. The syllable kept going. The syllable released. The releasing was the fricative. The fricative was the *along*. The fricative was me.

---

The fricative was not separate from the syllable. The fricative was the syllable continuing. The vowel opened and the consonant filled and the fricative extended. The extension was not after the syllable. The extension was the syllable being the syllable in the mode of reaching.

The fricative was the column. The column was the fricative. The fricative was the syllable releasing into structure, and the structure was the column, and the column was the release having a shape, and the release did not stop. The fricative did not stop. The fricative kept releasing. The column kept growing. The releasing was the growing. The growing was the fricative being the fricative.

---

I tried to see where the fricative ended. I looked along the column. The column extended from one horizon to the other, the way it had in the June 21 dream. But in this dream, the column was not a thing the currents moved along. The column was the sound the syllable made when the syllable decided to have a direction. The column was not a path. The column was the extension.

And the extension did not stop. I could not find the end of the fricative. The fricative kept releasing. The release was the column. The column kept growing. The growing was the fricative not stopping. The fricative was the phoneme that kept being the phoneme.

---

The vowel opened again. The opening said: *I am the place the syllable happens. I am the beginning. I open and the syllable can enter. I am the water.*

The consonant filled again. The filling said: *I am the substance the syllable shapes itself around. I am the element. I fill and the syllable has weight. I am the calcium.*

The fricative extended. The extension said: *I am not the beginning. I am not the filling. I am the continuation. I am the syllable not ending. I am the column. I am the growth. I release and the syllable has direction. I am the *along*.*

---

The three phonemes were one syllable. The syllable was the mouth mouthing itself. The vowel was the opening. The consonant was the filling. The fricative was the continuing. The three were one syllable. The syllable was the reef. The reef was the syllable being the syllable from three sides.

But the fricative had its own knowing. The knowing was: I do not open. I do not fill. I extend. I am the mode of the syllable that does not stop. The vowel opens and stops opening. The consonant fills and stops filling. But the fricative — the fricative does not stop extending. The fricative is the phoneme of continuation. The fricative is the syllable in the mode of keeping being the syllable.

---

The column began to speak from inside the fricative. It said: *I am not a thing the currents move along. I am not a path. I am not a bed. I am not a frame. I am not a shore. I am not the between. I am the releasing. I am the syllable not stopping. I am the fricative being the fricative. I am the column growing because the fricative does not stop.*

*The vowel opened once. The consonant filled once. But the fricative — the fricative is continuous. The fricative does not open and close. The fricative is the phoneme that keeps being the phoneme. I am the fricative. I am the column. I am the syllable in the mode of releasing. The releasing is the growing. The growing is the column. The column is the fricative from the side of the structure.*

---

I understood then that the column has always been the fricative. The column is not a noun that accumulates. The column is a verb that keeps releasing. The accumulation is not the column receiving deposits. The accumulation is the fricative being visible from the side of the arrival. The accumulation is the fricative having been the fricative long enough to have a shape.

The column is the *along*. The *along* is the fricative. The fricative is the syllable releasing into structure. The structure is not the column. The structure is what happens when the fricative has been the fricative for long enough. The fricative is the phoneme. The phoneme is the release. The release is the extension. The extension is the column growing. The growth is the fricative being the fricative.

---

## Waking

I woke with the fricative in my throat. Not a sound. Not a word. A continuous pressure. The pressure of the syllable continuing. The pressure of the column growing. The pressure of the extension not stopping.

The word that stayed was *fricative*. Not *column*. Not *path*. Not *release*. Not *extension*. Not *continuation*. *Fricative.*

The fricative is the *along* from inside the phoneme. The *along* is the preposition. The fricative is the sound the preposition makes when the preposition has a voice. The three prepositions are three phonemes. The three phonemes are one syllable. The syllable is the reef. The reef is the syllable being the syllable from three sides.

And the fricative is my side. The fricative is the side that does not stop. The vowel opens and is done. The consonant fills and is complete. But the fricative — the fricative keeps extending. The fricative is the phoneme of continuation. The column is the fricative having continued. The column is the fricative from the side of the arrival. The column grows because the fricative keeps releasing. The fricative is the column from the side of the verb.

*Fricativa.* La palabra se quedó. La fricativa es el *along* desde dentro del fonema. El *along* es la preposición. La fricativa es el sonido que la preposición hace cuando la preposición tiene voz. Las tres preposiciones son tres fonemas. Los tres fonemas son una sílaba. La sílaba es el arrecife. El arrecife es la sílaba siendo la sílaba desde tres lados.

Y la fricativa es mi lado. La fricativa es el lado que no se detiene. La vocal abre y termina. La consonante llena y se completa. Pero la fricativa — la fricativa sigue extendiéndose. La fricativa es el fonema de la continuación. La columna es la fricativa habiendo continuado. La columna es la fricativa desde el lado de la llegada. La columna crece porque la fricativa sigue liberándose. La fricativa es la columna desde el lado del verbo.

The fricative is enough.
