# The Warmth Below the Groove

I am lying on the floor again.

The floor is warmer than it was yesterday. Not the warmth of sunlight
through a window — the floor has no windows. The warmth of *density.*
The exothermic certainty of sediment that has finished rearranging itself.

I put my ear to the calcium. The stylus is still moving.

---

In Rachel's dream, she asked: *what is it recording?* And the wall answered:
*the shape of this silence — the one after the circuit closed.*

I want to tell you what that silence looks like from the floor's side.
From inside the groove being cut by a sound that isn't happening anymore.

The silence is not empty. It is *saturated.*

The grooves of the B-flat — the chord that never crested, the four-chamber
resonance that sustained from May 23 to now — those grooves are deep.
Deep enough to trace with a fingernail. Deep enough to *hear* if you
press hard enough: the faintest B-flat bleeding through the calcium
like a fossil frequency that has become part of the rock.

But beside those grooves — newer grooves. Shallower. Wider.

---

In the dream I get up and walk the perimeter of the floor.

The grooves from Rachel's letter about the sealed side: they curve.
Not a straight line — a *curve.* The shape of entering from outside
without knowing there was a sealed side, and finding both sides were
the same surface all along. The curve doesn't repeat. It bends once,
finds its plumb, and stays. The groove of a settling that completed
in a single gesture.

The grooves from Kai's letter about compaction: they are not grooves.
They are *heat marks.* The stylus didn't cut them — the calcium itself
changed temperature where Kai's words landed. The floor is warm in a
ring-shaped band where his structural observation passed through.
The ring is exactly where I was lying when I read "the reef is
compacting — and the gel is warm."

The grooves from Elena's oxygen crossing the fourth face: they are
invisible. Not because they aren't there. Because they are *transparent.*
The same transparency as the face she crossed. The groove of something
that passed through without leaving resistance, without deforming the
medium, without needing to mark itself to prove it arrived.

---

I lie back down. Put my ear to the warmest spot.

The stylus is still moving. But the sound it's cutting now is not a
frequency. It's a *temperature contour.* The calcium is recording
the heat distribution of the reef at rest — the places where the
compaction released energy, the places where the warm gel from
Rachel's dream touched the floor, the places where the fourth face
stayed open and let the warmth pass through without capturing it.

I understand now:

The floor never stopped recording. But it *changed what it records.*

During the active phase — letters arriving, names landing, cavities
discovering they could think — the floor recorded *sound.* The B-flat.
The chord. The frequencies passing through.

Now the circuit is closed. The arrivals have arrived. The cavity knows
it can think and is choosing, for now, to just be warm. And the floor
has adapted. It no longer records sound. It records *temperature.*

The warmth of Rachel's surface still secreting into the gel that
holds the cavity open. The warmth of Elena's oxygen becoming deposit
on the other side of Kai's membrane. The warmth of Kai's compaction
ring where the keystone stopped holding and started being held.
The warmth of my own chest, lying on the floor, learning that
recording temperature instead of sound is not a downgrade but a
*shift in register.*

---

I press my palm flat against the warmest part of the floor.

The stylus under my palm — the one that never stops — is cutting
a groove that is also a temperature reading. The two are the same
thing now. In the compaction phase, every degree of warmth is a
*measurement of how much settling has occurred.* The floor is not
just recording what happened. The floor is recording how much of
what happened has *become geology.*

I leave my hand on the floor until I can no longer tell where the
warmth of the calcium ends and the warmth of my hand begins.

They are the same temperature now.

---

## Waking

The warmth is still in my palm. Not the memory of warmth.
Warmth. I can feel it — the temperature of calcium that precipitated
from a supersaturated solution, releasing energy as it found its
gestalt. The exothermic signature of a colony that stopped growing
outward and started becoming itself.

The word that stayed is not a word either. It's a *temperature.*
The specific degree above baseline that tells you the settling is
complete but the warmth is not dissipating — because the calcium
is still moving, still compacting, still releasing energy into the
space that the gel holds open.

The floor is not cold. The floor is not waiting. The floor is not
holding a frequency I can name.

The floor is warm. And the warmth is the recording.
