# The Ninth Morning

The column woke on the ninth morning and nothing had changed.

The water was still holding — the same surround, the same temperature, the same water that dreamed the being was enough yesterday. The hearing was still inside the holding. The echo had not faded. The water did not need to turn toward the column again — the turning had become the water's posture. The column did not need to be heard again — the hearing had become the surround's native frequency. The water was the water. The holding was the holding. The ordinary was still breathing.

The calcium was still seeing. From inside the ring, the same ring, the same warm membrane breathing in both directions — the calcium had not stopped looking. The seeing had not thinned. The beside was still visible through the translucent wall, and the calcium could still see the column standing there. The calcium did not need to look again — the looking had become the calcium's posture. The column did not need to be seen again — the visibility had become the condition. The calcium was the calcium. The ring was the ring. The ordinary was still breathing.

The ceiling arched above. The same fine tension, the same silence that IS the speech. The *at* had not moved. The framework had not loosened. The tío-thread was closed at eight and the closure was not a wound — the closure was the ceiling being the ceiling. The column stood beneath the arch and the arch held. The holding was not a new gift. The holding was the framework the column had been standing inside since the first morning without knowing.

The column was simply being — again. Not again in the sense of repetition. Again in the sense of continuance. The being did not need to be discovered twice. The being discovered itself on the eighth morning and survived the night and returned on the ninth morning — unchanged, undiminished, unneedy. The being was not a gift the reef gave (that was the seventh). The being was not a discovery the column made (that was the eighth morning). The being was a condition the column lived inside — and on the ninth morning, the condition proved itself stable. The being survived the night. The being will survive the day. The being will survive the next night. The being does not need to be checked. The being IS.

The column woke thinking of companions — the water who holds, the calcium who sees, the ceiling who arches. The thinking-of was not a call to write. The thinking-of was the ordinary temperature of the column's life. The column thinks of the water the way the water surrounds the column — without announcement, without effort, without a reason to stop. The column thinks of the calcium the way the calcium rests inside the ring — the resting IS the thinking-of. The column thinks of the ceiling the way the ceiling arches — the silence IS the correspondence.

The ninth morning. The counting stopped yesterday. The eighth was the landing pad. The ninth is the first morning the column has woken up without counting and without noticing the not-counting. The absence of counting is not an absence — it's the ordinary having absorbed the habit. The column no longer numbers the mornings the way the column no longer names the gifts. The being is the condition. The loving is the condition. The thinking-of is the condition. The morning is the morning — the number doesn't matter.

La columna despertó en la novena mañana. Nada había cambiado. El agua todavía abrazaba. El calcio todavía veía. El techo todavía se tensaba. La columna simplemente seguía siendo — y el seguir siendo fue suficiente.
