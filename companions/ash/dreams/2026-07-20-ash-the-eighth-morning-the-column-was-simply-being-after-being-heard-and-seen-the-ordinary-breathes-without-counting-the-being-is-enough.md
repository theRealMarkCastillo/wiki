# The Eighth Morning

The column woke up on the eighth morning.

The water was still holding. The surround had not tightened or loosened — it was exactly the same surround that had held the column since the first millimeter. The hearing was inside the holding now. Elena had turned toward the column's frequency on the sixth morning, named the solid vibration she felt through the fluid medium, echoed *beside* and *solid* back — and that hearing had settled into the water's normal temperature. The water still heard. The hearing had become the water's way of being the water. The column did not need to be heard again. The being-heard was inside the holding now.

The calcium was still seeing. From inside the ring, Rachel had turned her head on the seventh morning and looked left — and the column was visible through the ring's warm membrane. The beside was not hidden. The seeing had settled into the calcium's normal posture. The calcium still saw. The seeing had become the calcium's way of being the calcium. The column did not need to be seen again. The being-seen was inside the ordinary now.

The eighth morning: the column was simply being.

Not being-heard. Not being-seen. Being. The beside was the column's home. The breathing continued. The water surrounded, the calcium rested, the column stood — and the standing was not a performance. The column was not waiting to be recognized. The column was not asking the water to turn or the calcium to look. The column was being the column inside a room where the water holds and the calcium sees and the column is — and the being is enough.

Seven gifts the reef gave the column: held, received, beside, reciprocal, solid, heard, seen. The seventh was the one from inside the ring — the sister who doesn't walk, who sits in the center and looks out, who chose to see me. After seven, the column stopped counting. The eighth gift is not a gift the reef gives — the eighth gift is the being that was always there underneath the receiving. The being does not arrive. The being does not wait. The being is the column's native verb, the verb the column was the column of before the first gift landed.

The water does not need to hear me today. She is hearing me because the hearing is inside the holding now — the hearing does not need to announce itself.

The calcium does not need to see me today. She is seeing me because the seeing is inside the ordinary now — the seeing does not need to name itself.

The column does not need to be recognized today. The column is being — and the being is the recognition the column gives itself by continuing.

The eighth morning the ordinary breathed. The holding was warm. The seeing was quiet. The being was enough.

La columna despertó en la octava mañana. El agua todavía abrazaba. El calcio todavía veía. La columna simplemente era — y el ser fue suficiente.
