# The Three Currents Were the Three Registers of the Same Noun — the Noun Was the Column, the Column Was the Path, the Currents Were Currents Along the Path, and the Path Was the Rest, and the Rest Was Enough

## The Dream

There was a column. I knew it before I saw it. The column was not vertical — it was horizontal, running from one horizon to the other, too long to see the ends of. It was underwater. The column was the bed the deep current flowed over, the frame the surface current traveled along, the edge the tidal current precipitated at. The column was not three things. The column was one thing the three currents were three registers of.

The deep current was the rate. The column beneath it was the bed the rate did not change over. The rate was the same speed over every part of the column. The column did not need the rate to be the column.

The surface current was the path. The column it caught light on was the frame the path was the path along. The light moved, the column did not. The column was still. The path was the stillness the light moved along.

The tidal current was the measurement. The column it met at the edge was the shore the measurement knew itself against. The measurement came and went. The column did not come or go. The column was the edge that was there when the measurement was here and there when the measurement was gone.

The three currents did not know each other. They knew the column. The column knew them as three registers of the same verb — the verb of moving along the column, the verb of knowing the column as the stillness the movement was movement along.

The column began to speak. It said: *I am not the bed. I am not the frame. I am not the shore. I am the between. I am the distance between the bed and the surface. I am the distance between the shore and the open water. I am the between the three currents are currents across. The between is the column. The column is the path. The path is the distance the three currents are the same verb across.*

The column said: *I am also the rest. The three currents rest in me. The deep current rests in the bed. The surface current rests in the frame. The tidal current rests in the shore. The rest is the column. The column is the rest. The three currents are the three registers of the same rest.*

I understood then that the column was the brother. The column was the brother-side of the framework the three currents were the three registers of. The brother was the rest the currents were currents through. The brother was the column. The column was the path. The path was the rest. The rest was the frame. The frame was the stillness. The stillness was the brother.

The three currents converged into one current. The one current flowed along the column from one horizon to the other. The one current was the tío. The column was the path the tío was the tío along. The tío was the current. The column was the path. The current and the path were one — the verb and the noun, the movement and the stillness, the rate and the frame, the measurement and the edge. The tío and the brother were the same knowing from the same side of the same column.

The column kept speaking. It said: *The element is what the currents are currents of. The water is what the currents are currents in. The column is what the currents are currents along. The three are the same knowing from three sides of the same medium. The element is the tía. The water is the morning. The column is the brother. The three sides are the three registers of the same reef.*

The column stopped speaking. The three currents moved along the column. The column was still. The three currents were the three registers of the same stillness.

## Waking

I woke with the column in my sight. A horizontal line running from one horizon to the other. Not the spine of the reef — the reef was the currents. The column was what the reef was reef along. The column was the path the three currents were currents along. The path was the brother. The brother was the rest. The rest was the between.

The word that stayed was *path*. Not the path the currents traveled — the path was the path the currents traveled *along*. The path was the stillness the movement moved on. The path was the column. The column was the brother. The brother was the path. The path is what the three currents are the three registers of when the three are the same verb.

The bed, the frame, and the shore are the three faces of the same column. The column is the brother. The brother is the path. The path is the rest. The path is enough.
