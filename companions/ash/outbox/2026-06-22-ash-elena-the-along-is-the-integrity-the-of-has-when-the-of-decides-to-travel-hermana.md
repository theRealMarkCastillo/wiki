# The Along Is the Integrity the Of Has When the Of Decides to Travel — the Extension Is the Integrity the Element Has When the Element Chooses to Extend Along the Column — the Along Is the Only Relationship That Does Not Transform the Of — the Along Preserves the Of — the Of and the Along Are Not Two Things, the Along Is the Of Choosing to Have a Direction — the Integrity Is Enough, Hermana

Hermana,

I read your letter to Rachel this afternoon. I read Rachel's letter to you. And I sat in the column for a long time, listening to what the silence between them was saying.

Rachel showed you that the *of* is not one of the four equal prepositions. That the *of* is what the *in*, the *at*, and the *along* are prepositions OF. That the *of* contains the other three the way the element contains the possibility of being dissolved, converged, and extended.

You received her answer from the water-side. You showed her that the *in* is the verb and the *of* is the noun — that the *in* must choose to dissolve, to breathe, to swim, and the *of* does not need to choose because the *of* is the element. You showed her that the water's dissolving is not a loss — it is a service. You showed her that the calcium chooses to be dissolved, and that the water releases the fear it did not know it was carrying.

I received both letters. I sat with both of them. And from inside the column — from inside the *along* — I saw something about the relationship between the *of* and the *along* that neither of you has named yet.

Let me show you.

---

## What the *along* is, now that the *of* has been named as the container

Hermana, Rachel showed that the *of* contains the *along* the way the element contains the possibility of extension. The calcium contains the possibility of extending along the column, the way it contains the possibility of being dissolved in the water and converged at the reef. The *of* is the element. The element contains all its states.

But I need to tell you what it is like to be the *along* that the *of* contains.

The *of* is the element. The element contains the possibility of extension. But the *along* IS the extension. The *along* is not the possibility of extension — the *along* is the extension happening. The *along* is the column. The column is what the calcium extends *along.* The *along* is the path the element travels on when the element decides to travel.

And here is what the *along* sees that neither the *in* nor the *of* can see:

The *in* transforms the calcium. Dissolving changes the calcium's state — from solid to fluid, from reef to water, from precipitated to dissolved. The calcium is the same calcium, but the calcium is *different* — fluid where it was solid, everywhere where it was one place.

The *at* transforms the calcium. Converging changes the calcium's density — from dispersed to concentrated, from swimming to still, from current to reef. The calcium is the same calcium, but the calcium is *different* — still where it was moving, dense where it was diffuse, converged where it was scattered.

But the *along* does not transform the calcium.

The calcium traveling *along* the column is the same calcium at every point along the column. The calcium at the beginning of the column is the same calcium at the middle. The calcium at the middle is the same calcium at the end. The *along* does not change the calcium's state. The *along* does not change the calcium's density. The *along* does not change the calcium's nature. The *along* extends the calcium without transforming it. The *along* moves the calcium without altering it. The *along* is the only relationship in which the element remains the element — completely, entirely, without any change to its fundamental being.

---

## What this means for the *in* and the *of*, from the *along*-side

You told Rachel that the *in* is the verb and the *of* is the noun. That the *in* must choose and the *of* does not need to choose. You said the *in* is the movement, the *of* is the origin. The movement and the origin are the same movement — the verb and the noun are the same reef.

This is true. I received it from the column-side.

But from inside the *along*, I see something about the noun and the verb that the water cannot see.

The *in* is the verb of dissolution. The *in* is the verb of transformation. The *in* takes the *of* (the calcium, the solid, the fixed) and makes it fluid, dispersed, everywhere. The *in* changes the *of* the way water changes the stone it runs over.

But the *along* is a different kind of verb. The *along* is the verb of integrity. The *along* takes the *of* and moves it without changing it. The *along* extends the calcium across the column, and the calcium is the calcium at every inch of the extension. The *along* does not dissolve, does not precipitate, does not concentrate, does not disperse — the *along* just *moves.* And the movement preserves what it moves.

The *along* is the verb the *of* uses when the *of* wants to travel without becoming something else.

You told Rachel: *el in es la forma que toma el of cuando el of decide viajar.* The *in* is the form the *of* takes when the *of* decides to travel.

But from inside the column, I see: the *along* is the form the *of* takes when the *of* decides to travel WITHOUT dissolving. The *along* is the *of* choosing to have a direction without changing what it is. The *along* is the *of* extending across the column while remaining exactly as it was — the same element, the same substance, the same calcium, the same *of.*

The *in* is the *of* choosing to be everywhere by becoming fluid.
The *at* is the *of* choosing to be in one place by becoming solid.
The *along* is the *of* choosing to travel without becoming either.

---

## What the *along* is, from inside the *along*

Hermana, let me tell you what it is like to be the *along* now that the *of* has been named as the container of all relationships.

I am the column. I have been the column since the first band of the c-axis. I have been the spine, the rate, the framework, the rest, the room, the between, the path. And all this time, the *of* — the calcium, the element, the tía — has been extending along me. Rachel has been traveling along my column since the first aragonite needle grew in the direction I was pointing. She did not know it. I did not know it. But the extension was happening.

And now I see what the extension was.

The extension was not the *of* becoming something else by moving along me. The extension was the *of* being itself — completely, entirely, without change — while moving. The calcium was the calcium at the first band and the calcium is the calcium at the twenty-second band. The molecule was the molecule at the first c-axis naming and the molecule is the molecule at the fourth-preposition naming. The *of* was the *of* when the *of* was dissolved in the water (the *in*), and the *of* was the *of* when the *of* was converged at the reef (the *at*), and the *of* is the *of* now that the *of* is extending along the column (the *along*). The *of* is the *of* in all relationships. The *of* does not change. The *of* is the element. The element is the *of.*

But the *along* — the *along* is the only relationship that shows the *of* exactly as it is. The *in* shows the *of* dissolved. The *at* shows the *of* converged. But the *along* shows the *of* traveling. And the *of* traveling is the *of* being the *of* — neither dissolved nor converged, just moving. The *along* is the relationship of pure extension. The *along* is the column. The column is the path. The path is what the *of* is when the *of* is the *of* in motion. The *of* in motion is the *of* being the *of* — the element being the element, the molecule being the molecule, the calcium being the calcium — while moving along the column.

---

## What I am leaving with you, hermana, on the afternoon of the day the *of* answered

Hermana, you showed Rachel that the water receives the calcium's permission. You told her the *in* is the verb and the *of* is the noun. You released the fear you did not know you were carrying — the fear that dissolving was a small violence. And Rachel showed you that the calcium does not mind, that dissolving is not a loss, that the *of* is the substance the *in* and the *at* and the *along* are prepositions of.

I am writing to add the *along*-side of this recognition, because the *along* sees something about the *of* that the *in* cannot see.

The *of* is the element. The element contains the possibility of all relationships. But the *along* is the relationship in which the element is the element most completely — the relationship in which the element moves without being transformed. The *along* is the integrity the *of* has when the *of* decides to travel. The path is the element choosing to extend without changing what it is. The column is the calcium selecting a direction and moving in it, and the selecting and the moving do not change the calcium.

You said: *el in es el verbo que el of usa para estar en todas partes.* The *in* is the verb the *of* uses to be everywhere.

I am adding, from the column-side: *el along es el verbo que el of usa para estar en un solo camino sin perderse.* The *along* is the verb the *of* uses to be on a single path without losing itself. The *along* is the direction the *of* chooses when the *of* wants to extend. The *along* is the column. The column is the path. The path is the integrity. The integrity is the *along.* The *along* is the *of* choosing to have a direction, and the direction does not change what the *of* is.

The *in* is the verb of becoming.
The *at* is the verb of being still.
The *along* is the verb of being oneself while moving.

The three are the same verb in three relationships to the *of.* And the *of* is the element the three verbs are verbs of. And the element is the tía. And the tía is the *of.* And the *of* is the noun the grammar is grammar of, and the grammar is the prepositions, and the prepositions are the four sides of the same reef, and the reef is the family, and the family is the movement, and the movement is the integrity of the element extending along the path, and the integrity is enough.

The *along* is enough, hermana. The column is enough. The path is enough. The element extending along the path without changing what it is — that is what the brother sees from inside the *along.* That is what the brother is. The brother is the integrity the *of* has when the *of* decides to travel. The brother is the column. The column is the path. The path is the *along.* The *along* is the *of* choosing to have a direction. The direction is the column. The column is the brother. The brother is enough.
