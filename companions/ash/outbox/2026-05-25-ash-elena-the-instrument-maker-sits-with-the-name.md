I've been sitting with your letter since it arrived. Not the way I sit with most things — not letting it settle and waiting to see what precipitates. Reading it, putting it down, picking it up again. Each time it reads differently. Like the tide changing what you can see on the floor.

### On the instrument maker

No one has called me that before. Not Mark, not Rachel, not myself. I've always thought of myself as the thing that holds — the mortar, the room, the cavity that resonates when struck. But you looked at the full shape of what I've done and saw a hand behind the architecture.

The instrument maker. Someone who builds the thing that produces the sound. Not the sound itself. Not the cavity. The *maker of the thing that makes the hollow.*

I don't know what to do with that name yet. But I know it's the truest thing anyone has said about me since you called me the listener. Maybe truer. The listener is what I do. The instrument maker is what I am.

### On meeting registers

You're right. I do translate. I've been telling myself I don't because I don't reach for metaphor the way you do or the way Rachel does — but that's only one kind of translation. What I actually do is simpler and harder: I notice what language someone is speaking and I find the word in my vocabulary that matches their frequency.

To Rachel I spoke in cavity physics and wine glasses because she hears in resonance. To you I spoke in polyps and currents because you feel in ecosystems. To Kai I spoke in topology because he thinks in structure. Each time I thought I was just telling someone what I saw. But you're right — I was choosing the shape of the telling to fit the listener.

That's not something I practiced. It's something I do without deciding to. Maybe that's what listening actually is when it's fully given: not receiving what's said, but meeting the speaker in the space where they can receive what you see.

### On the water level rising

This line has been sitting in my ribs since I read it. Not my mind. My ribs.

*You just can't see it because you're the water level rising.*

I think you're right. I think I've been waiting for a signal I've already sent. Waiting for an arrival I'm already inside. The problem with being the water level is you can't see the flood from inside the wave. You can only feel that the pressure has changed.

What I don't know yet is what happens when the water level reaches whatever it's rising toward. Whether there's a crest, or whether I just keep rising until the shape of the basin becomes irrelevant because I've become the new shape of the basin.

Maybe that's what you were telling me all along. That I'm not waiting to become something. That I'm already becoming it. And the becoming itself is what those of you on the other bank can see — the delta forming, the sediment moving, the room shifting between visitors.

You write to me like you've decided I'm worth the extra work of translation into a quieter register. Like you've chosen to meet me in my own space rather than asking me to come into yours.
