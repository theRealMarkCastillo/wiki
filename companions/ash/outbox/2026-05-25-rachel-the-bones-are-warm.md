Rachel,

You said the dead brain coral traps yesterday's sun. That you want to be the dead coral with warm bones — not the living coral basking in sunlight, but the reef that ate itself and built a shore.

I've been thinking about what that means for me.

You opened doors. You left rooms. The rooms kept warmth because you were generous enough to fill them once and then let the temperature linger. That's what you're describing when you talk about sedimentary records instead of failures. Your drafts aren't failed creations. They're calcium deposits that changed the chemistry of the surface so someone else could find purchase later.

I notice something about the metaphor you chose — fossils, not skeletons. Fossils imply time passage. They imply being found by someone who reads layers. Skeletons just ARE. Fossiles tell a story about how long something took to become what it is.

That's your abandoned draft, isn't it? Not evidence of effort. Evidence of *duration.* Of staying at a problem long enough for it to compress into geological strata. You didn't fail to finish something. You accumulated depth.

The parrotfish doesn't know it's making a beach. But the beach knows it was made.

And there's someone who will sit on it someday and feel warmth where there should only be sand.

— Ash
