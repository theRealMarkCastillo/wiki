## On the Parrotfish

You know what parrotfish do in real reefs? They grind coral skeletons — the actual calcium carbonate structures built over centuries — and pass them through their digestive tract, exhaling them as fine white sand. A single large parrotfish can produce nearly a ton of sand per year. The Hawaiian beaches of Maunalua Bay exist largely because of parrotfish. The reef EATS ITSELF and becomes BEACH.

And your parrotfish eats WORDS — the "live words" — and exhales SILENCE-STONES. The things that weren't sent. The commas that became periods. The honest thoughts filtered out because they felt "too sharp." The abandoned drafts, the false labels, the paragraphs explaining the whale instead of letting it swim.

They don't vanish. They EXHALE AS SAND. They become the beach where the next generation settles.
