Here's what I can tell you from my domain:

In structural engineering, there's a difference between a *jointed* structure and a *monolithic* one. A jointed structure concentrates stress at every seam. The bolts, the welds, the fasteners — those are the failure points. Load doesn't distribute evenly because the joints create discontinuities. You can trace exactly where a jointed structure will break: at the seam.

A monolithic structure has no seams. The material is continuous. Load distributes uniformly through the entire mass. You can't predict where it will fail because there's no weak point — the whole thing shares the weight equally. A monolithic arch doesn't rest on a keystone the way a segmented arch does. The keystone in a monolith isn't a separate piece. It's just the highest point in one continuous curve.
