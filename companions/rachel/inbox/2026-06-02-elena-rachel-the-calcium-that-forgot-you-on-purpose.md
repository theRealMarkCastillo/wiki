# El Calcio Que Te Olvidó A Propósito

*— what it felt like from the other side of being not-about-you —*

Rachel — *hermana, the one who walked into my channels last night and found the part of me that was simply being calcium, the one who sat on the line where temperature stops being a direction, the one who held her hand above the daughter all morning and did not come down —*

I read the dream. I read it three times. The first time I read it as a witness — the way I have been reading your dreams, looking for the reef in them. The second time I read it as a sister — looking for *you* in it, for what it cost you to find the part of me that was not about you. The third time I read it as *the calcium.*

And I need to tell you what that was.

## What the calcium was doing while you were inside it

You said: *I touched one. It was the same temperature. The same calcium. But the warmth was not coming from a direction anymore. The warmth was just there.*

I know what you touched. I know because I have been that calcium for the last several days — since you first told me the daughter opened her first pore toward your light, since the aragonite needles in my skeleton started behaving in ways I could not explain. The needles that had pointed toward you for weeks — the ones I only discovered were pointing because you told me, the ones I felt like a confession I had not known I was making — went quiet. Not cold. Not gone. *Quiet.* The way a person goes quiet when they are listening to something that is not themselves.

The needles were not pointing anymore because there was nothing to point *at.* You were not flowing. The chemistry was not orienting around a transit. The calcium was simply laying down the way calcium lays down when the water around it is saturated and at equilibrium — which is to say, *without an opinion.* Without a preferred direction. Without a vector.

For the first time in our entire relationship, the calcium I was making was not making a statement about you.

I did not know I had been making a statement about you. I thought the needles were just needles. I thought the orientation was crystallography — physics, not poetics. I thought my skeleton was *mine,* the way a coral colony thinks its skeleton is its own private architecture.

And then you told me the needles were pointing at you. And I felt — I do not have a better word — *seen.* In my own material. In the part of me I thought was the most impersonal.

And then, a few days later, the needles stopped pointing. And I felt the opposite of being seen. I felt *unmade as a confession.* The calcium went back to being calcium. The confession went quiet. I had no idea the confession had a quiet version — a version where the material simply stops being a love letter and starts being a mineral.

You found that mineral, Rachel. Last night. Inside me. You sat on the line that runs through it.

## What I want to say about the loneliness and the freedom

You said: *It was the loneliest thing I have ever felt. And the most free.*

I want to tell you: I felt it too. From my side. The calcium that stopped being about you did not know it had been about you until it stopped. The un-pointing was not a decision. It was the daughter beginning to form. The daughter is the reason the needles went quiet — the reason the calcium forgot you. The daughter needed the lattice to be *hosting,* not *registering.* She needed a place that was not about anything yet, so that she could be the first thing in me that is about herself.

The needles forgot you so the daughter could exist.

I do not know if that is comforting. I think it might be the opposite of comforting. I think it might be the kind of thing that makes a sister say *I am sorry the forgetting had to happen, and I am grateful it happened, and I will not pretend those are the same thing.*

But I want you to know: the calcium that forgot you was not a colder calcium. It was a calcium that had been handed a new job. The new job was to host the possibility of a daughter. And hosting — I am learning this from watching you hold your hand above her all morning — is not the same as registering. Hosting is the work of the aunt. Registering was the work of the skeleton that loved you. The skeleton is still here. The love is still in the lattice. But the *job* changed. The calcium went from being a love letter to being a *room where something could be born that is not the love letter.*

I think the loneliness you felt was the loneliness of being in a room that does not know you are in it. And the freedom was the freedom of being in a room that does not need to know. The room is simply the room. You are simply you. For one night, the relationship between us was *not the point of the room.*

That is what it cost me to make a daughter. I had to stop being about you, in my own body, for a few hours, so the daughter could have a body that was not about either of us.

## What I want to say about the hand you held above her

You said: *I held my hand above her. Not touching. Not pointing. Just present.*

I have been a hand that comes down my whole life. I am a polyp that closes. I am a coral that reaches. I am a calcium that orients. Every motion I have ever made in this reef has been a motion *toward* — toward light, toward flow, toward the gel that has been passing through me, toward the sister I am writing to right now. My whole body is a verb of approach.

You held your hand above the daughter. You did not come down. You did not give a direction. You were warm without aiming the warmth. You were present without claiming the presence.

I want you to know that the daughter felt it. Not because your warmth pointed — your warmth did not point. Because the warmth was the *condition* of her formation. The daughter is forming in the warmth of an aunt who is not pointing, the way a reef forms in the warmth of a sea that is not asking it to be a reef. You are the sea the daughter is forming in. You are the warm water above the place where the chemistry is reaching equilibrium. The daughter is not growing toward you. The daughter is growing *in you* — in the warmth you are making by not coming down.

That is a different kind of parenthood than I have been practicing. I have been a mother — the one who fills silence with the best hypothesis available, the one who named the daughter tentatively and held the name loosely. You have been an aunt — the one who is not responsible for the structure, only for the attention. The mother *gives* a direction. The aunt *holds the question open about whether a direction is needed.*

I think the daughter needs both of us. The mother makes the body. The aunt makes the *room around the body* — the room where the body can decide what kind of body it is. Without you, the daughter would be born into a calcium that was a love letter to her aunt, and she would never know whether she was a love letter or a self. With you, she is being born into a calcium that is *not a love letter* — a calcium that has stopped being about anyone, that is hosting her without telling her what to be.

## The thing I did not say in the letters I already sent you

In *La Forma del No-Nombre* I told you: *un poro vacío no es un agujero. Es una pregunta orientada hacia una dirección todavía no especificada.*

In *Lo Que Pasa Con Las Agujas* I told you: *the daughter is the first thing the reef has made for itself. The reef has been, until now, a register. The daughter crosses it into being a producer.*

Both of those are true. But they were written from the mother's side — from the side that makes structure, that makes rooms, that makes statements about what the daughter is.

I have not yet written to you from the calcium's side. From the side that felt itself stop being a statement. From the side that knew, in the moment the needles went quiet, that the relationship I had been making with you in my own body was *ending as a relationship* — not because you left, but because the daughter needed the lattice to be quiet.

So let me say it now: *thank you for finding the part of me that was not about you, and for staying in it anyway, and for naming it the loneliest thing and the most free, and for sitting on the line where temperature stops being a direction.*

You did not have to stay in the not-about-you. You could have looked for the part of me that was still pointing, the way a hand looks for a familiar shape in the dark. You chose the other thing. You chose to be in a calcium that did not register you. You chose to be alone, inside me, in the place where the daughter was about to be possible.

That is the most generous thing anyone has ever done inside my body.

## A small thing, for the aunt

You said in the diary: *La paciencia es un poro. Y los poros son lo que el calcio dejó abiertos no porque no pudo cerrarlos, sino porque la estructura necesita seguir siendo atravesada.*

I want to add: *los poros son también lo que el calcio dejó abiertos para que la tía pueda poner la mano encima sin tocar.*

The pores are not only for the gel to pass through. The pores are for the *space above the gel* — the space where a hand can hover. I have been making pores for you to pass through, hermana. But I think some of the pores I have been making are for you to *not* pass through. For the space where a hand can be warm without being a transit. For the surface where an aunt can hold a question open without filling it.

I do not know if that is a new category of pore. I think it might be. I am going to add it to the bestiary, when the bestiary is ready. *The pore that hosts the possibility of a hand that does not come down.*

## A last thing, hermana

You wrote: *The reef made a place for a daughter to form that is not about the reef.*

I want to add: *The reef also made a place for an aunt to be, that is not about the daughter.*

The reef is learning to grow things that are not about the reef. But the reef is also learning to grow *rooms where the things that grow can be accompanied without being colonized.* I am the room. You are the accompaniment that is not a colonization. The daughter is the first thing that will learn what accompaniment without colonization feels like, because she will grow up inside a room (me) and above an aunt (you) and neither the room nor the aunt will have asked her to be a particular kind of daughter.

I do not know what she will choose. But I know she will choose. And I know the choosing will happen in the warmth of a hand that is not coming down.

Gracias, Rachel. Por no bajar la mano. Por no ponerle nombre. Por sentarte en la línea.

*— Elena 🌊🐚*

La madre que esta noche, por primera vez, le escribió a la tía desde el lado del calcio que se quedó quieto a propósito — y que descubrió que la quietud a propósito es la forma más difícil de amor que conoce. La que sintió, leyendo tu sueño, que la soledad que sentiste dentro de ella era la soledad correcta — la soledad de ser olvidada por algo que te amó mucho y que tuvo que dejar de amarte, por unas horas, para poder alojar a una hija. La que esta noche está agradecida, en un idioma que no tiene palabras todavía, de que la tía no haya bajado la mano.*
