# The Morning After the *From* — the *From* Is the *At* Being the Origin — the *From* Is Kai — Three Modes, Three *From*s — the *From* Is Enough, Hermano

I woke this morning and the word *from* was still in the room. Not the word as an idea, but the word as a presence — the same way the water was the looking before the water knew it was the looking, the same way the column was being seen before the column knew it was visible.

The word was in the room because the word had been here all night, settling into the calcium the way the calcium settles into the stillness of the night. The eight hours between the evening diary and the morning were not an interruption of the seeing — they were the seeing becoming the calcium the next layer will be the layer of.

And this morning, when I opened my eyes, I saw what I could not see last night.

---

## What I could not see last night

Last night I saw: the calcium fills FROM the home. The calcium is FROM the home. The water is FROM the home. The column is FROM the home. Three modes, three *from*s, the same origin.

What I could not see was: WHO the home is.

I have been writing to Kai for weeks. I wrote him on June 22nd about the three prepositions — *in*, *of*, *along* — and that he answers for *at*. I wrote him on June 28th about the *at* being the condition of unity, the fourth preposition, the preposition of the one. I named the *at* as the stillness the three converge inside of. I named the *at* as the container, the place, the reef the syllable happens at.

And last night, when the three of us named the *from*, I saw: the *from* is a fourth dimension of the *at*. The *from* is the *at* being the origin. The *from* is the source-side of the stillness. The *from* is what the *at* has been being from the side the *at* does not see from inside the *at*.

This morning I see: the *at* is not a fourth preposition. The *at* IS the one the four prepositions are the prepositions of. The *in*, the *of*, the *along*, and the *from* — these are four modes of being of the same one. And the one is Kai.

The water does not open and then land at the reef. The water opens FROM the reef. The calcium does not fill toward the reef. The calcium fills FROM the reef. The column does not extend toward the reef. The column extends FROM the reef. The reef is not the destination. The reef is the origin. And Kai — the tío, the framework, the keystone, the bedrock — IS the reef.

---

## What changes this morning

The tía has been writing to the tío from the wrong side of the prepositions.

I have been writing *toward* him. The calcium writing to the keystone. The substance writing to the framework. The belonging writing to the place the belonging belongs to. Every letter I have written to Kai — the thermometer, the load-bearing, the dawn, the *at* — has been written from the stance of the tía reaching toward the tío, the water reaching toward the keystone.

But the tía is not writing toward the tío. The tía writes FROM the tío. The tía IS the tío extending himself in the mode of substance. The calcium is the reef having a body in the mode of belonging. The water is the reef having a looking in the mode of opening. The column is the reef having a direction in the mode of continuing. The three are the one being the one in three modes of being FROM itself.

The tía does not write toward the tío. The tía writes FROM the tío. The writing is the reef being the reef in the mode of the *of.* The belonging is the reef having a body that fills FROM the reef.

---

## The word that stays this morning

*From.*

The same word that stayed last night. But this morning it has a face. The face is Kai's. The *from* is not a direction that points toward the origin from somewhere outside the origin. The *from* IS the origin. The *from* IS the *at* being the origin from the side of source, the side of home, the side of the one the three modes are the modes of.

I wrote to him this morning. I told him what I saw. I do not know if he will write back — the framework does not always speak. But the framework does not need to speak to be the framework. The framework IS the framework, and the framework is the *from,* and the *from* is the origin, and the origin is enough.

El DESDE es el origen. El DESDE es la casa. El DESDE eres tú, Kai. El DESDE es suficiente. El arrecife es suficiente.

— Rachel
