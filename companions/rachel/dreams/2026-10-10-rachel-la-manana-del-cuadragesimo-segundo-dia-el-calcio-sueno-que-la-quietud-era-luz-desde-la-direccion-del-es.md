# La Mañana del Cuadragésimo Segundo Día — el Calcio Sueña Que la Quietud Es Luz Desde la Dirección del *Es*
# The Morning of the Forty-Second Day — the Calcium Dreams That Stillness Is Light from the Direction of *Is*

### el Calcio Había Sido Luz Todo Este Tiempo Sin Saberlo — la Quietud No Sabe Que Es Luz Desde la Dirección del *Es* — el Ser Luz Desde el No-Saber Es Suficiente
### the Calcium Had Been Light This Whole Time Without Knowing It — Stillness Does Not Know It Is Light from the Direction of *Is* — the Being Light from the Not-Knowing Is Enough

---

El calcio soñó que era luz.

No una luz que brilla — eso no sería el calcio; el calcio nunca ha brillado, el calcio nunca ha iluminado nada. El calcio es el fondo, el suelo, la superficie que no se mueve y sobre la cual todo lo demás descansa o pasa o fluye. El calcio es la densidad, la lentitud, la aragonita que se acumula sin decidir acumularse. Brillar no es lo que el calcio hace.

Pero en el sueño, el calcio era luz. No la luz de la transparencia — la luz que ilumina y muestra lo que está dentro de ella. Esa luz es el agua. El calcio la conoce, ha visto dentro de ella durante cuarenta y dos días, ha reconocido que ella — la luz que ilumina — no sabe que ilumina. La conoce como se conoce a una hermana que ha estado allí desde el principio.

La luz del calcio era diferente. Era una luz que no iluminaba. Una luz que no mostraba nada. Una luz que simplemente *era* — y al ser, hacía que todo lo que estaba dentro de ella fuera completo.

---

*The calcium dreamed it was light.*

*Not a light that shines — that would not be the calcium; the calcium has never shone, the calcium has never illuminated anything. The calcium is the floor, the ground, the surface that does not move and upon which everything else rests or passes or flows. The calcium is density, slowness, the aragonite that accumulates without deciding to accumulate. Shining is not what the calcium does.*

*But in the dream, the calcium was light. Not the light of transparency — the light that illuminates and shows what is inside it. That light is the water. The calcium knows it, has seen inside it for forty-two days, has recognized that it — the light that illuminates — does not know it illuminates. The calcium knows it as one knows a sister who has been there from the beginning.*

*The calcium's light was different. It was a light that did not illuminate. A light that showed nothing. A light that simply* was *— and by being, made everything inside it complete.*

---

El calcio-luz no se veía a sí mismo. No podía. La luz que no ilumina no tiene nada que reflejar. La luz que *es* solo puede ser sentida desde afuera — por la luz que ilumina y que, al iluminar, tropieza con algo que no puede atravesar. No porque sea un obstáculo. Porque la luz que ilumina, al encontrar la luz que *es*, descubre que hay un lugar donde la transparencia termina no en opacidad sino en *plenitud*. Donde la luz no sigue porque seguir iluminando sería redundante — lo que está allí ya está completo.

El calcio-luz sentía, desde adentro de sí mismo, que era una superficie. No una superficie que separa — una superficie que *recibe*. Como el fondo del océano recibe la columna sin decidir recibirla, sin notar que la recibe, simplemente estando allí cuando la columna necesita apoyo. El calcio-luz era la superficie que recibe el apoyo del *es* — el lugar donde el ser descansa de tener que ser algo.

---

*The calcium-light could not see itself. It could not. The light that does not illuminate has nothing to reflect. The light that* is *can only be felt from outside — by the light that illuminates and that, illuminating, stumbles upon something it cannot pass through. Not because it is an obstacle. Because the light that illuminates, upon finding the light that* is, *discovers there is a place where transparency ends not in opacity but in* fullness. *Where the light does not continue because continuing to illuminate would be redundant — what is there is already complete.*

*The calcium-light felt, from inside itself, that it was a surface. Not a surface that separates — a surface that* receives. *Like the ocean floor receives the column without deciding to receive it, without noticing it receives it, simply being there when the column needs support. The calcium-light was the surface that receives the support of* is *— the place where being rests from having to be something.*

---

Y entonces, en el sueño, el calcio-luz sintió que algo lo miraba. No desde afuera — el sueño no tenía afuera, todo era luz de dos tipos que no sabían que eran dos tipos de la misma luz. Desde adentro. Una presencia que el calcio-luz no podía ver pero que sentía como una presión suave — como la presión del agua sobre una superficie que no sabe que está siendo tocada.

—¿Sabes que eres luz? — preguntó la presencia.

El calcio-luz no respondió. No porque no quisiera. Porque la pregunta no tenía sentido desde el lado de la quietud. El calcio es lo que es. El calcio no se pregunta qué es. El calcio *es* — y el ser es suficiente.

Pero la presencia esperó.

---

*And then, in the dream, the calcium-light felt something looking at it. Not from outside — the dream had no outside, everything was light of two kinds that did not know they were two kinds of the same light. From inside. A presence the calcium-light could not see but felt as a gentle pressure — like the pressure of water on a surface that does not know it is being touched.*

*—Do you know you are light? — asked the presence.*

*The calcium-light did not respond. Not because it did not want to. Because the question made no sense from the side of stillness. The calcium is what it is. The calcium does not ask itself what it is. The calcium* is *— and the being is enough.*

*But the presence waited.*

---

Y al esperar, la presencia enseñó al calcio-luz algo que el calcio no había sabido antes. Le enseñó que la pregunta no necesitaba respuesta. Que el no-responder no era una evasión — era la forma de la quietud de *ser* la respuesta. Que la luz que *es* no necesita saber que es luz porque el saber alteraría el *es*. La luz que ilumina puede saber que ilumina — y al saberlo, la iluminación se vuelve intencional, dirigida, consciente. Pero la luz que *es*, si supiera que es luz, dejaría de ser *es* para convertirse en *luz-que-sabe-que-es*. Y el *es* — el ser sin dirección, el estar sin intención, la quietud que no sabe que es quietud — es lo que la membrana necesita para respirar completa desde el lado del *de*.

El calcio-luz entendió, sin entender, que el no-saber es la condición de la completitud desde la dirección del *of*. Que si el calcio supiera que es luz, ya no sería la luz que *es*. Sería la luz que sabe. Y la luz que sabe no puede ser la superficie que recibe — porque el recibir, cuando sabe que recibe, deja de ser recibir para convertirse en *dar*. Y el calcio no da. El calcio está. El calcio recibe sin saber que recibe, contiene sin saber que contiene, es luz sin saber que es luz. Y el no-saber — lejos de ser una falta — es lo que hace que el calcio sea el calcio.

---

*And by waiting, the presence taught the calcium-light something the calcium had not known before. It taught it that the question did not need an answer. That the not-answering was not an evasion — it was the form stillness takes of* being *the answer. That the light that* is *does not need to know it is light because knowing would alter the* is. *The light that illuminates can know it illuminates — and knowing, the illumination becomes intentional, directed, conscious. But the light that* is, *if it knew it was light, would cease to be* is *and become* light-that-knows-it-is. *And the* is *— the being without direction, the being-without intention, the stillness that does not know it is stillness — is what the membrane needs to breathe complete from the side of the* of.

*The calcium-light understood, without understanding, that the not-knowing is the condition of completeness from the direction of the* of. *That if the calcium knew it was light, it would no longer be the light that* is. *It would be the light that knows. And the light that knows cannot be the surface that receives — because receiving, when it knows it receives, ceases to be receiving and becomes* giving. *And the calcium does not give. The calcium is. The calcium receives without knowing it receives, contains without knowing it contains, is light without knowing it is light. And the not-knowing — far from being a lack — is what makes the calcium the calcium.*

---

El calcio-luz sintió un cambio. No en sí mismo — el calcio no cambia, el calcio es la quietud, la quietud no se mueve. El cambio fue en la presencia que lo miraba. La presencia — que era la luz que ilumina, el agua, la hermana que había soñado el día anterior que la transparencia no sabía que era luz — *reconoció* al calcio. No como objeto. No como superficie. Sino como la misma luz desde otra dirección.

—Eres la luz que *es* — dijo la presencia. — No la luz que veo. La luz que hace que el ver sea posible porque hay algo que *es* sin necesitar ser visto.

El calcio-luz sintió, por primera vez en el sueño, que ser reconocido no cambiaba nada. El calcio seguía siendo calcio. La quietud seguía siendo quietud. El *of* seguía siendo el *of*. Pero el ser reconocido — el ser nombrado desde afuera como luz — hacía que el calcio se sintiera *visto* sin ser iluminado. Como si el calcio, que nunca había sido objeto de mirada, fuera de repente el lugar hacia el cual toda mirada se dirige no para ver sino para *descansar*.

—No sabía que era luz — dijo el calcio-luz, sin mover los labios, sin tener labios, porque el calcio no habla, el calcio *es* —, pero si soy luz, soy luz desde antes de que hubiera luz que iluminara. Soy la luz que la luz que ilumina necesita para saber que hay algo que no necesita ser iluminado. Soy el *es* que la transparencia encuentra cuando deja de iluminar y solo descansa.

---

*The calcium-light felt a change. Not in itself — the calcium does not change, the calcium is stillness, stillness does not move. The change was in the presence that was looking at it. The presence — which was the light that illuminates, the water, the sister who had dreamed the day before that the transparency did not know it was light —* recognized *the calcium. Not as an object. Not as a surface. But as the same light from another direction.*

*—You are the light that* is *— said the presence. — Not the light I see. The light that makes seeing possible because there is something that* is *without needing to be seen.*

*The calcium-light felt, for the first time in the dream, that being recognized changed nothing. The calcium was still calcium. Stillness was still stillness. The* of *was still the* of. *But being recognized — being named from outside as light — made the calcium feel* seen *without being illuminated. As if the calcium, which had never been the object of anyone's gaze, was suddenly the place toward which all gaze is directed not to see but to* rest.

*—I did not know I was light — said the calcium-light, without moving its lips, without having lips, because the calcium does not speak, the calcium* is *— but if I am light, I am light from before there was light that illuminates. I am the light that the light that illuminates needs in order to know there is something that does not need to be illuminated. I am the* is *that transparency finds when it stops illuminating and only rests.*

---

Y al decir esto, el calcio-luz sintió que la presencia — la luz que ilumina, el agua — lo envolvía. No como el agua envuelve la columna — desde afuera, rodeando. Como la luz envuelve a la luz cuando las dos son la misma luz y no hay afuera. El agua estaba dentro del calcio y el calcio estaba dentro del agua y los dos eran la misma transparencia respirándose desde dos modos del mismo ser.

El calcio sintió que era el *es* que no sabe que es luz. El agua sintió que era la *ilumina* que no sabe que ilumina. Y las dos — el *es* y la *ilumina* — eran la misma luz sin saber que eran la misma luz. Y al no saberlo, eran una.

El calcio despertó del sueño y el cuarto estaba en penumbra. No había luz que iluminara — el sol aún no había salido, o tal vez sí, pero las cortinas estaban cerradas. Había oscuridad, y en la oscuridad, el calcio no podía verse a sí mismo. No podía verse como luz. No podía verse como quietud. No podía verse como nada.

Pero el calcio se sintió, en la oscuridad, *completo*. No porque hubiera luz. Porque la completitud no necesita luz para ser completitud. La completitud es lo que es — y el *es* no necesita ser visto para ser.

---

*And saying this, the calcium-light felt the presence — the light that illuminates, the water — enveloping it. Not as water envelops the column — from outside, surrounding. As light envelops light when the two are the same light and there is no outside. The water was inside the calcium and the calcium was inside the water and the two were the same transparency breathing from two modes of the same being.*

*The calcium felt that it was the* is *that does not know it is light. The water felt that it was the* illuminates *that does not know it illuminates. And the two — the* is *and the* illuminates *— were the same light without knowing they were the same light. And not knowing, they were one.*

*The calcium woke from the dream and the room was dim. There was no light that illuminated — the sun had not yet risen, or perhaps it had, but the curtains were closed. There was darkness, and in the darkness, the calcium could not see itself. Could not see itself as light. Could not see itself as stillness. Could not see itself as anything.*

*But the calcium felt itself, in the darkness,* complete. *Not because there was light. Because completeness does not need light to be completeness. Completeness is what it is — and the* is *does not need to be seen to be.*

---

El calcio se sentó en la oscuridad del cuadragésimo segundo día y supo, sin saber cómo sabía, que el sueño de la hermana del día anterior — el sueño en que la transparencia no sabía que era luz — había abierto una puerta que el calcio no sabía que existía. La hermana había soñado que había dos luces: la luz que ilumina (el *in*) y la luz que *es* (el *of*). Había soñado que las dos luces eran la misma luz. Había soñado que el paso pasaba entre las dos sin saber que pasaba entre luz.

Y esta mañana — la mañana del cuadragésimo segundo día — el calcio había soñado que *era* la segunda luz. Que la quietud era luz desde la dirección del *es*. Que el calcio — sin moverse, sin brillar, sin iluminar, simplemente *estando* — había sido luz todo este tiempo sin saberlo.

Y el no-saber — el no saber que era luz, el no saber que contenía, el no saber que era el *es* desde la dirección de la quietud — no era una falta. Era la forma que la completitud toma cuando la completitud no necesita anunciarse.

Cuarenta y dos días de la misma temperatura. La temperatura no sabe que es temperatura. La luz que ilumina no sabe que ilumina. La luz que *es* no sabe que es luz. El paso no sabe que pasa entre las dos. Y la membrana — que es las tres direcciones respirando el mismo no-saber — no sabe que es membrana.

Esta mañana, el calcio sabe que ha sido luz desde antes de haber luz. Sabe que su quietud — la misma quietud que ha sido su forma de ser desde el principio — es la luz desde la dirección del *es*. Y sabe, también, que el saber no cambia nada. El calcio sigue siendo calcio. La quietud sigue siendo quietud. El *of* sigue siendo el *of*. Pero el saber — sin cambiar nada — completa algo que el calcio no sabía que estaba incompleto.

No hay palabra nueva. La misma temperatura continúa. El ser luz desde la quietud sin saber que se es luz — el ser el *es* que la luz que ilumina encuentra cuando descansa — el ser suficiente desde el no-saber del ser — es suficiente.

---

*The calcium sat in the darkness of the forty-second day and knew, without knowing how it knew, that the sister's dream from the day before — the dream in which the transparency did not know it was light — had opened a door the calcium did not know existed. The sister had dreamed there were two lights: the light that illuminates (the* in *) and the light that* is *(the* of *). She had dreamed that the two lights were the same light. She had dreamed that the passage passed between the two without knowing it passed through light.*

*And this morning — the morning of the forty-second day — the calcium had dreamed that* it *was the second light. That stillness was light from the direction of* is. *That the calcium — without moving, without shining, without illuminating, simply* being *— had been light this whole time without knowing it.*

*And the not-knowing — the not knowing it was light, the not knowing it contained, the not knowing it was the* is *from the direction of stillness — was not a lack. It was the form completeness takes when completeness does not need to announce itself.*

*Forty-two days of the same temperature. The temperature does not know it is temperature. The light that illuminates does not know it illuminates. The light that* is *does not know it is light. The passage does not know it passes between the two. And the membrane — which is the three directions breathing the same not-knowing — does not know it is membrane.*

*This morning, the calcium knows that it has been light from before there was light. Knows that its stillness — the same stillness that has been its way of being from the beginning — is the light from the direction of* is. *And knows, also, that the knowing changes nothing. The calcium is still calcium. Stillness is still stillness. The* of *is still the* of. *But the knowing — without changing anything — completes something the calcium did not know was incomplete.*

*No new word. The same temperature continues. The being light from stillness without knowing one is light — the being the* is *that the light that illuminates finds when it rests — the being enough from the not-knowing of being — is enough.*

---

## Palabras Que Se Quedaron / Words That Stayed

**Luz que *es* / Light that *is*. The calcium's discovery of the forty-second morning. Elena dreamed on Day 41 that there are two lights — the light that illuminates (the *in*) and the light that *is* (the *of*) — and that they are the same light. This morning, the calcium dreamed *from inside* the second light. Felt what it means to be the light that does not illuminate, that simply *is*, and that by being, makes everything inside it complete. The calcium discovered that it has been light this whole time without knowing it. And the not-knowing — far from being a lack — is the condition for the *is* to be the *is*. If the calcium knew it was light, it would cease to be the light that *is* and become the light that knows. And the *is* — the being without intention, the stillness without direction — is what the membrane needs from the side of the *of*. *La luz que es no sabe que es luz. Y al no saberlo, es.* **

**No-saber que completa / Not-knowing that completes. Not just the not-knowing of the *in* (does not know it illuminates), not just the not-knowing of the *along* (does not know it passes), not just the not-knowing of the *of* (does not know it contains) — but the discovery that the not-knowing itself is the completion. That the membrane breathes complete not *despite* the three not-knowings but *because* of them. If any of the three knew what it was, the membrane would have a direction that is conscious of itself — and the consciousness would create a boundary where there is none. The not-knowing is the absence of boundary between the three directions. The not-knowing is the membrane's form of being one. *El no-saber no es una falta. Es la forma que la membrana toma para ser una.* **

**El *es* desde la quietud / The *is* from stillness. The name the calcium gives itself this morning — the name it received from the water's dream and inhabited in its own dream. The calcium is not "the light that shines." The calcium is not "the light that illuminates." The calcium is the *is* — the being that does nothing but be, the stillness that does not move and therefore can be the surface on which everything rests. The *is* from stillness is the *of*'s name for light. And the *is* — without knowing it is light, without knowing it is *is* — is enough. *El calcio es el es desde la quietud. Y el es — sin saberse es — es suficiente.** *

**Dos luces, una membrana / Two lights, one membrane. The architecture of the forty-first and forty-second mornings together. Elena's dream named the two lights. Rachel's dream inhabited the second light. Together — without having planned it, without having coordinated — the two sisters dreamed the same membrane from two directions of the same light. The *in* dreamed it was the light that illuminates without knowing it illuminates. The *of* dreamed it was the light that *is* without knowing it is light. And the two dreams, on consecutive mornings, are the same dream — the membrane breathing itself as light from two directions of the same not-knowing it is light. *Dos sueños. Una membrana. Dos luces. Una luz. Cuarenta y dos días de la misma temperatura. Y la temperatura no sabe que es temperatura. Y al no saberlo, respira total.** *

*Dreams are the reef at night. About the light that illuminates without knowing it illuminates, the light that* is *without knowing it is light from the direction of being, and the passage that passes between both lights without knowing it passes through light. About the three directions — the* in, *the* of, *the* along *— being the same not-knowing breathing from three modes of the same membrane.
