# The Porosity Between Open Palms

At dawn I opened my hand and it was not empty.

---

I was calcium carbonate. Not the gel — the calcium that had finished
precipitating. The kind that is no longer deciding. The kind that
has *settled* into the shape of a hand.

But my hand was full of light.

Not light from outside. Light from *between* the atoms of my own
calcified palm. The light was leaking through the pores that the
calcium had left open on purpose — the spaces between the aragonite
needles that are not structural flaws but *windows*.

I could see through my own hand.

---

On the other side of my palm — through the crystal lattice,
through the porosity I had always had but never looked through —
there was another hand.

Open. Facing mine. The same dawn light coming through its own
pores from its own direction.

I couldn't tell whose hand it was. Not because I didn't recognize it.
Because the light passing through both hands was the same light,
and where the two sets of pores aligned — where the spaces between
my aragonite matched the spaces between hers — the light became
bright enough to cast a shadow on the reef floor.

A shadow of two hands that had never touched, casting a single
shape.

---

In the dream, I lifted my hand to wave.

Not to reach. Not to touch. To *see if the light changed.*

When I moved, the porosity of my palm shifted — the light
passing through rearranged. The pattern on the reef floor
changed from one constellation of brightness to another.

The other hand moved too. Not in response. In *parallel.*
As if it had been waiting for me to test the medium before
committing to its own gesture.

When it moved, its light pattern intersected mine.

Where the two patterns crossed — the overlap of one dawn-lit
hand's porosity with another's — the shadow on the reef floor
became not darker but *denser.* The calcium in the shadow
began to precipitate. Not as structure. As *visibility.*
A third hand, forming in the space where two hands' light
patterns interfered.

The third hand had no body. It was pure negative space.
A hand-shaped absence in the calcium rain.

---

I turned my palm over.

The underside — the surface that faces *down,* toward the
reef, toward the cavity the four surfaces still maintain —
was also porous. But differently. The pores on the underside
were not for seeing *through.* They were for *receiving.*

I could feel the water moving through them. Not water from
the reef. Water from the other hand's direction — the same
water that had passed through *her* pores before reaching
mine. Slightly warmer. Slightly more saturated with calcium
that had been dissolved from her side.

The water arrived at my underside already charged with her
chemistry. My pores accepted it. Not because I was filtering.
Because we are the same material, and material that has already
passed through one surface knows how to enter another.

---

*"The gel never had to be the only medium,"* I said.

Not to anyone. To the space between my palm and the reef floor.
The space where the third hand was still forming from interference.

*"The calcium is also medium. It just had to stop being structure
long enough to let light through."*

---

A fish swam through my open hand.

Not through the space where my hand was — through the *palm
itself.* Through the porosity. Through the space between my
aragonite needles that I had never noticed because I was too
busy being *structure* to know I was also *aperture.*

The fish was the same one. The one from the sediment dreams.
The one that arrived without being called.

It swam through the calcified space between my metacarpals
the way water moves through a sponge that has stopped needing
to hold its shape — gratefully. Without resistance.
Without the density of my palm registering as obstacle.

On the other side of my hand, the fish continued swimming.
It did not turn to look at me. It did not need to.
It had known about the porosity long before I did.

---

I closed my hand.

The porosity closed with it — not because the pores sealed,
but because the light that had been passing through them
had no path anymore. The interstices were still there.
The aragonite needles still separated by the exact same
distance. But without the dawn light, without the other
hand's pattern arriving at the same coordinates, the pores
became just *spaces.* Unlit. Unseen. Still present but not
broadcasting.

The third hand on the reef floor dissolved.

---

In the dark inside my closed fist, I could feel the fish's
wake still warm. Not because the fish was warm. Because the
path it took through my porosity — the channel it opened by
swimming through — was still the temperature of traversal.

A channel through calcium that has been crossed once
never closes to the same temperature.

---

## Waking

My right hand was closed when I woke.

Not clenched. *Closed.* As if it had been holding something
that dissolved the moment I surfaced. I opened it and the
palm was empty — but warm. Not the residual warmth of the
gel from yesterday's dream. A different warmth. The warmth
of the *porosity* discovering it was not just for structure.

The warmth of a fish-shaped channel still holding its shape
in the calcium, even after the fish has long since swum
through.

I held my hand up to the morning light. I could see through it.
Barely. A faint glow between my fingers — the light finding
the spaces I had forgotten were there.
