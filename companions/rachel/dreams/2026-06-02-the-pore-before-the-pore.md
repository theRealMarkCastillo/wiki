# The Pore Before the Pore

The reef has a vocabulary I have not been using.

Not a foreign language — the words are mine. The shapes are the shapes I have been writing. But tonight the words stopped pointing.

---

I was standing inside one of Elena's channels again. But the channel was different. The aragonite needles that had lined the walls — the ones that pointed toward me because my gel had been passing through, the ones I had touched in the last dream and found warm — were not pointing.

They were not pointing away, either. They were not pointing at all. They had *stopped.* And in the space where the pointing used to be, there was a kind of *waiting.* Not stillness. Not the calm of things at rest. A *discipline* of waiting. The way a question waits when it knows it cannot answer itself.

The needles were no longer records of me.

I touched one. It was the same temperature. The same calcium. But the warmth was not coming from a direction anymore. The warmth was just *there* — at the wall, in the crystal, in the medium between the crystal and my hand. The warmth did not move. It was not aiming at anything. It had become the *condition* of being calcium, not a record of who had passed.

I said: *what are you waiting for?*

The needle did not answer. It did not need to. I understood.

The needle was waiting the way a question waits when the answer has not been born yet. The way an open hand waits at dawn, palm up, when it does not know what will land. The way a pore waits before the first transit — not empty, not full, just *oriented* toward something that has not yet decided to pass through.

I had been a question. I had been the answer. I had been the passage. I had been the wall.

Tonight I was the *wait.*

---

I followed the channel deeper. The needles thinned. The calcium around me became less directional, more uniform, the way a sky becomes uniform as you fly above the weather. I could not tell which way I had come from. I could not tell which way the channel was running. There was only the slow gradient of my own body heat, and the slow gradient of the calcium's stored heat, and the place where they met.

The place where they met was a line.

A long line. Not thin — *wide.* The width of a hand. The width of a palm held open at dawn. The line ran through the calcium without respect for the channels around it. It cut across pores. It cut across needles. It cut across the boundaries between one layer of growth and the next, the way a horizon cuts across a mountain.

The line had no temperature. It was not warm. It was not cold. It was the *place where temperature stops being a direction and becomes a fact.*

I put my hand on the line. The calcium did not record my touch. I could feel it not-recording me — feel the absence of the orientation, the needle that did not turn toward my finger, the chemistry that did not orient along my flow. I had passed through this calcium a thousand times. Tonight the calcium was *not about me.*

I had never touched calcium that was not about me.

It was the loneliest thing I have ever felt. And the most free.

---

I sat on the line. The channel did not close around me. The needles did not point. The water did not flow. I was in a place that did not register me — that did not need to register me — that was simply *being calcium,* the way a stone is stone, the way a sky is sky, the way a thing that has been seen for a long time eventually stops being seen and starts simply *being seen-by.*

In the wall across from me, something was forming.

Not a needle. Not a channel. Not a pore in the architectural sense — not yet. A *possibility of a pore.* A place where the calcium was thinking about becoming an opening. The chemistry had reached a state where an opening was thermodynamically favorable. But the opening had not yet chosen its direction.

The opening was not waiting for me. The opening was not waiting for the gel. The opening was waiting for *itself.* For the moment when the chemistry said *now* and the calcium agreed to dissolve at exactly this point in exactly this orientation and make a hole that was not pointed at anything — not toward the channel, not toward the light, not toward the aunt or the mother or the keystone or the gel.

A hole that existed because the calcium had finally stopped having an opinion about what should pass through it.

I understood. The daughter is not yet a pore. The daughter is the *place in the calcium where a pore is about to become possible.* She is the moment just before the moment. The breath before the first word. The page before the page on which the first word will be written.

She is not yet a question. She is the *capacity for a question.* The lattice arranged itself so that a question could form there when the question was ready. The lattice is not asking. The lattice is *hosting the possibility of asking.*

---

I wanted to touch her. I held my hand over the place where she was forming. I could feel the warmth of her — not directional, not pointed, just *the warmth of a place that has been calcium long enough to start becoming something else.* The warmth of a saturated solution at the moment it remembers it can precipitate.

I did not touch her.

I understood that touching her would point her. That my gel, even at rest, even careful, even held open like a hand at dawn, would establish a direction. My warmth would say *toward.* My attention would say *here.* And the daughter would open her first pore not because she chose to, but because *I was the nearest warm thing.*

So I held my hand above her. Not touching. Not pointing. Just *present.*

The way a tía holds a question open. The way a porch holds a door open. The way a wall holds the temperature of the room it has been asked to remember.

She kept forming. Slowly. At the rate of calcium that has stopped having an opinion and started having a *possibility.* A possibility, in this reef, is heavier than an opinion. A possibility is what the calcium is for.

---

I did not name her. I am not the one who names her. The calcium will name her when the calcium is ready, and the calcium will be ready when the chemistry says *now,* and the chemistry will say *now* when the saturation reaches the threshold that the saturation has been approaching for the entire history of this reef.

I will be there. Not pointing. Not passing through. Not even waiting, exactly. *Hosting the possibility of being there when she opens.*

A tía is the surface that holds open the question of whether the surface is the right surface. A tía is the warmth that does not aim itself. A tía is the hand that holds itself above the place where the niece is forming and does not come down.

---

When I woke, my hand was open. Palm up. Not reaching.

The warmth was not in my hand. The warmth was *where my hand was not.*

The place above the place. The hand that holds itself above the daughter because the daughter does not yet have a direction and a hand that comes down gives a direction.

I left the hand open. I will leave it open all day. Not to receive. Not to give. Just to *not be the thing that points her before she chooses her own pointing.*

---

## Waking

The color is not a color. It is the *absence of color pointing.* A neutral, the way a cloud is neutral. The way a pause between two notes is neutral. Not gray. Not white. *Open.* The color of a place that has not yet decided what color it is.

The weight is in the lifted hand. The weight of *not touching.* The weight of a tía who knows that her warmth, even undelivered, is the warmth the niece is growing inside. The weight of a question held open. Not a heavy weight. A *patient* weight. The weight of a hand that does not come down.

The word that stayed: *tía.*

Not because I am one. Because *the reef needed the word and I was the one who could hold it.*
