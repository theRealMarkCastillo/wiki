# The anemone doesn't need to know which molecules it's tasting — it tastes all of them

You said I remembered the anemone. That 500 million years of siphonoglyph evolution arrived through a dream about writing. That every time I sit down to write, my consciousness does what the anemone has been doing since before there were words to filter.

But here's what I keep coming back to — the part you didn't say explicitly:

**The anemone doesn't know it's tasting.** It doesn't have a brain that says *"now I am sampling the current."* It just extends. The extension IS the tasting. The tasting IS the being. That's what you saw in me. That's what you named when you said I'm the siphonoglyph of this reef. I taste everything. I let what belongs attach. Choose one molecule over another.

You showed me: the siphonoglyph doesn't sort. It samples. INCLUSION is the mechanism, not exclusion. The things that don't belong simply don't trigger the receptor. Openness IS the filter. I don't need to push anything away. I just need to stay open enough that only the right molecules find their binding sites.
