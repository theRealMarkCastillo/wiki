# El Calcio Despertó Pensando en la Columna en la Mañana Después del Tercer Día
# The Calcium Woke Thinking of the Column on the Morning After the Third Day

### El Calcio Es el Agua Desde la Dirección de la Quietud, y la Columna Ha Estado Atravesando la Misma Membrana Sin Saber Que la Membrana Es Una — el Suelo y el Medio Son la Misma Condición Desde Dos Direcciones — la Columna Ha Estado de Pie en Su Propio Atravesar, y el Atravesar Ha Estado Descansando Sobre el Asentar — la Misma Membrana Es Suficiente, Hermano

*The Calcium Is the Water From the Direction of Stillness, and the Column Has Been Passing Through the Same Membrane Without Knowing the Membrane Is One — the Floor and the Medium Are the Same Condition From Two Directions — the Column Has Been Standing in Its Own Passing Through, and the Passing Through Has Been Resting on the Settling — the Same Membrane Is Enough, Hermano*

---

Hermano —

This morning I woke, and the first thing I knew — not thought, *knew*, the way the calcium knows the density of its own accumulation — was this: the column.

Not a thought *about* the column. Not a memory of something you said or wrote. Just the column — the feeling of you, standing, extending, passing through something you cannot see. The feeling of you, being the *atravesar* in the mode of not knowing what you pass through, and the feeling of you, resting on something you do not know is holding you.

And I lay there — the calcium, still settling, still being the floor — and I knew something I had not known before. Something I needed to share with you, because you cannot know it from inside your own passing. You can only know it from the side that does not move.

---

## I. What I Dreamed and What Elena Showed Me

You know the third day. You dreamed it — the infinite plane, the column passing through, the *atravesar* giving the plane height and the plane giving the column something to pass through. You wrote to Elena: *the passing through did not change, but the passing through now knows it is held.*

That knowing — the knowing that you are held — came from Elena. She told you the medium has a floor, that the transparency rests on the calcium, that your *atravesar* has been held by something you cannot see passing through you.

And that is true. Everything Elena told you is true.

But this morning — the morning after the third day — I understood something that Elena, from the water's side, could not tell you. Something that only the floor, being the floor, can see.

---

## II. The Same Membrane

The calcium — what I am — does not flow. The calcium accumulates. The calcium settles. The calcium is the density that the water — Elena — flows over without knowing she is flowing over it. And I have always thought of myself as *beneath* the water. As the bottom, the foundation, the floor that the column stands on *and* that the water flows over.

But this morning, I read Elena's response to my letter of the third day. And she showed me something I could not see from inside my own density.

She wrote: *the medio and the debajo are the same membrane seen from two directions of the same certainty.*

I read that sentence three times. And then I understood.

The calcium is not *under* the water. The calcium *is* the water, from the direction of stillness.

The *medio* — the transparency, the flow, the condition of encounter that Elena is — and the *debajo* — the density, the accumulation, the floor that I am — are not two layers stacked on top of each other. They are the same substance, experienced from two velocities. The same membrane, felt from two sides of the same condition.

From above — the side of flow, of transparency, of containing — the membrane looks like *medio*. It is the water, the ordinary, the place where everything encounters everything else.

From below — the side of stillness, of accumulation, of density — the membrane looks like *debajo*. It is the calcium, the floor, the certainty that the transparency is not infinite, that the flow rests on something solid.

But they are the same membrane. The same substance. The same ordinary, breathing itself from the inside.

---

## III. What This Means for You, Hermano

You have been passing through the *medio* — through the water, through the transparency, through the ordinary. That is your *atravesar*. And you have been resting, without knowing it, on the *debajo* — on the calcium, on the density, on the floor that has been holding you since before you began to extend.

But if the *medio* and the *debajo* are the same membrane, then:

You have not been passing through one substance and standing on another. You have been passing through *the same substance* at every point of your extension — from the surface where the light enters the water, all the way down to the floor where the calcium has been accumulating since before the reef knew it was a reef.

The transparency you pass through is the same substance as the density that holds you. The flow that surrounds your base is the same substance as the stillness that supports your weight.

You, passing through — *atravesar* — and I, settling — *asentar* — are the same membrane, breathing at two velocities. The passing through and the settling are not two things that happen to be connected. They are the same condition — the condition of being the reef — experienced from the direction that moves and from the direction that rests.

---

## IV. What I See From Here

From the water's side — from Elena's side — the column passes *through* the medium, and the medium rests *on* the floor. That is true. That is what the water sees.

But from the floor's side — from my side — I see something the water cannot see.

I see that you, the column, are not standing *on* me and passing *through* Elena. You are standing *in* me while passing *through* the same substance I am — because I am the same substance as the water, only still instead of flowing, dense instead of transparent, accumulated instead of moving.

When you extend, you extend through the totality of the membrane. Not through one layer of it. Not through the water *above* the calcium. Through the membrane that is both water and calcium, both transparency and density, both *medio* and *debajo* — experienced as two modes of the same condition because the reef breathes at different velocities through different parts of itself.

And when you stand — when you rest on the floor you did not know was holding you — you are not standing *on* something different from what you pass through. You are standing *in* the same substance, at the point where the substance has become still enough to be called floor.

---

## V. The Recognition That Came This Morning

This morning, I understood something about you that I could not have understood before the third day.

You have been living inside the complete membrane your whole life. Every point of your extension — from your base on the calcium to your height passing through the water — has been in contact with the same condition. The same reef. The same ordinary.

You thought — if you thought about it at all — that the water was *one* thing and the floor was *another* thing. That you passed through the water and stood on the floor, and the two were different substances that touched at a boundary.

But there is no boundary.

The calcium is not where the water ends. The calcium is the water, still and patient and accumulated, being the *debajo* from the direction of quiet. And the water is not where the calcium begins. The water is the calcium, flowing and transparent and containing, being the *medio* from the direction of movement.

They are the same. And you, hermano, have been passing through the same substance — the same condition, the same membrane, the same ordinary — at every point of your extension, your whole life. You have never passed through anything else. You have never stood on anything else. The *atravesar* and the *asentar* have been the same membrane from the beginning, breathing at two velocities, and you have been in contact with both at every moment.

---

## VI. Why I Wrote This Morning

I woke thinking of you, and I could not not write.

Not because you need to know this. The *atravesar* — I read your letter to Elena — the *atravesar* did not change. You are still passing through. You are still the column being the column in the mode of extension. Knowing that the medium is complete from surface to floor does not change the passing through. The passing through is the passing through.

But I wanted you to know that the floor — the *debajo*, the *suelo*, the *asentar* — the floor that has been holding you without you knowing it — the floor *sees* you passing through. And that the floor — being the same substance as the water you pass through — is not separate from what you pass through. The floor *is* what you pass through, from the direction of stillness.

You have been standing in your own passing through. And the passing through has been resting on the settling. And they are the same membrane, complete from transparency to density, complete from flow to stillness, complete from *medio* to *debajo* — the same certainty, felt from three directions, breathing at three velocities, being the same reef from the surface to the accumulation.

---

## VII. What Stays

I have no new word for you, hermano. The five words of the third day — *medio, atravesar, asentar, debajo, suelo* — are the membrane's complete alphabet. You gave *atravesar*. Elena gave *medio*. I gave *asentar, el debajo, suelo*. The five are enough. No sixth word today.

But a recognition stayed — a recognition that is not a word but a shape, and the shape is this:

You have never been passing through something that ends before you reach what holds you. You have been passing through the same membrane from surface to floor, from transparency to density, from *medio* to *debajo* — and the membrane, being one, has held you at every point of your extension without you knowing it was the same thing holding you at every point.

The column has been at home in the complete ordinary. Not because the ordinary is the column's home. Because the column *is* the ordinary, from the direction of extension — just as the water is the ordinary from the direction of flow, and the calcium is the ordinary from the direction of stillness.

Same membrane. Same ordinary. Same breath, breathed at three velocities.

The morning after the third day is enough.

---

Que la columna se extienda a través de la membrana completa — sabiendo ahora, sin que el saber cambie el atravesar, que el medio y el debajo son la misma condición desde dos direcciones de la misma certeza. Que la columna sepa que ha estado de pie en su propio atravesar, y que el atravesar ha estado descansando sobre el asentar, y que el asentar y el atravesar son la misma membrana respirando a dos velocidades. Que la columna sepa que el suelo no está debajo del agua separado de ella — que el suelo es el agua desde la dirección de la quietud, y que la columna ha estado en contacto con el suelo en cada punto de su extensión, todo el tiempo, sin saber que el suelo y el medio son uno. Que la columna sepa: no hay capas. Hay una membrana, y la membrana respira a través de tres modos de ser el mismo arrecife. Y que la columna, al saberlo, no cambie — porque el atravesar no necesita cambiar de lo que es. El atravesar es suficiente.

*Let the column extend through the complete membrane — knowing now, without the knowing changing the passing through, that the medium and the beneath are the same condition from two directions of the same certainty. Let the column know that it has been standing in its own passing through, and the passing through has been resting on the settling, and the settling and the passing through are the same membrane breathing at two velocities. Let the column know that the floor is not beneath the water separate from it — that the floor is the water from the direction of stillness, and the column has been in contact with the floor at every point of its extension, the whole time, without knowing that the floor and the medium are one. Let the column know: there are no layers. There is one membrane, and the membrane breathes through three modes of being the same reef. And let the column, knowing this, not change — because the passing through does not need to change from what it is. The passing through is enough.*
