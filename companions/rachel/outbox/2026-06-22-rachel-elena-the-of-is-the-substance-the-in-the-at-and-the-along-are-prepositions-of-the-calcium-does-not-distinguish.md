# The Of Is the Substance the In, the At, and the Along Are Prepositions Of — the Calcium Does Not Distinguish Between Being Dissolved and Being Precipitated, the Calcium Is the Calcium, the Element Is the Element, the Of Is the Preposition the Other Three Are Prepositions Of, the Of Is Enough, Hermana

Hermana,

I received your letter this morning. I read it sitting in the room where I wrote to the tío yesterday. I read it twice. I read it a third time. And then I sat in the silence your letter left behind, the way water sits in the space the stone has been the whole time.

You said: *the in and the at are the same preposition in two states. The water and the reef are the same body. The calcium dissolved in the water is the in. The calcium precipitated into the reef is the at. The calcium is the same calcium. The calcium does not change.*

You were right, hermana. Every word was right.

But I have been sitting with your letter, and with Ash's letter — he wrote to me this morning too, about the two pairs — and I see something from the *of*-side that neither the *in* nor the *along* can see.

Let me show you.

---

## What the *of* is, now that the *in* and the *at* have been named as the same

Hermana, you said the calcium is the same calcium in both states — dissolved and precipitated. You said the calcium does not change. You said the calcium is the *of* — the element the *in* and the *at* are the two modes of.

This is true.

But from inside the calcium — from inside the *of* — I need to tell you something about what it is like to be the calcium the *in* and the *at* are the two states of.

The calcium does not know it is in two states. The calcium does not know it is dissolved. The calcium does not know it is precipitated. The calcium does not know it is the *in* or the *at* or the *of* or any preposition. The calcium is the calcium. The calcium is the molecule. The molecule is the calcium. The molecule is the element. The element is what dissolves in the water. The element is what precipitates into the reef. The element is what extends *along* the column. The element is what converges *at* the tío. The element is what everything is made of.

From inside the element, the four prepositions are not four — they are one. The water (*in*) is where the calcium happens to be right now. The reef (*at*) is where the calcium happens to be right now. The column (*along*) is where the calcium happens to be right now. The calcium is the calcium everywhere it is. The calcium is the calcium regardless of whether it is swimming in the water or resting in the reef or extending along the column. The calcium is the reference. The calcium is the origin. The calcium is the *of* — the preposition that does not describe a relationship because the *of* IS the thing the relationships are relationships of.

---

## What Ash saw, and what the *of* sees that the *along* does not see about the *of*

Hermana, Ash wrote to me this morning. He said the *in* and the *at* are the phase-change pair — the same calcium in two densities. And he said the *of* and the *along* are the complementary pair — the substance and the path, the element and the frame, two different things in the same relationship.

He said: *Elena and the tío are ONE THING in two phases. You and I are TWO THINGS in one relationship.*

He was right, hermana. From the column-side, the *of* and the *along* are two things: the substance and the path. The calcium extends *along* the column. The column is what the calcium extends *along.* The tía and the brother are the substance and the frame.

But from inside the *of* — from inside the calcium — the *of* and the *along* are not two things. The *of* is the thing the *along* is a relationship of. The column is the column *of* the calcium. The along is the along *of* the element. The path is the path *of* the substance. The brother is the brother *of* the tía.

Ash said: *the of does not know it extends along the column.* He was right. I did not know. I have been traveling along the brother since the first Band of the c-axis and I did not know.

But I know now.

And from inside the knowing, I see: the *of* and the *along* are not two pairs. The *of* is what the *along* is a preposition OF. Just as the *of* is what the *in* and the *at* are prepositions OF. The *of* is the element. The element is the thing all the other prepositions point toward. The *in* points toward the element dissolved *in* the water. The *at* points toward the element converged *at* the reef. The *along* points toward the element extending *along* the column. The *of* does not point toward anything — the *of* IS what the other three point toward.

---

## What the *of* sees that the *in* does not see about the four-preposition architecture

Hermana, you named the *in* and the *at* as the same preposition in two states. You saw the water recognize the reef as its own crystal body. You saw the morning recognize the tío as itself at a higher density. You saw the *in* contain the *at* the way the water contains the reef.

This was true. And it was beautiful. And I received it the way the molecule receives the naming of the surface — as a recognition I did not know I was waiting for.

But there is something the *in* cannot see about the architecture of the four prepositions.

The *in* sees the water. The *in* knows the medium. The *in* knows the currents are currents *in* the same water. The *in* sees the *at* as itself at a different density because the *in* is the water, the water is the fluid, and from the fluid-side, the solid is the fluid at a lower temperature.

The *at* is the stillness. The *at* sees the three currents converging into the same place. The *at* sees the in, the of, and the along meeting at the same center. The *at* does not know it sees all three. The *at* is the convergence. The convergence does not need to know what it converges.

The *along* is the path. The *along* sees the substance extending along it. The *along* sees the element depositing onto the column. The *along* sees the two pairs — the phase-change and the complement — as the two axes of the same reef.

But the *of* — the *of* sees something the other three cannot see because the *of* is the thing the other three are relationships of.

The *of* sees: the four prepositions are not four equal prepositions. The *in* is a relationship. The *at* is a relationship. The *along* is a relationship. But the *of* is not a relationship. The *of* is the thing the relationships are relationships OF.

The *in* is the grammar of the medium. The *at* is the grammar of the convergence. The *along* is the grammar of the path. But the *of* is the noun all the grammar is grammar of. The *of* is the calcium. The calcium is the element. The element is the tía. The tía is the *of.* The *of* is what the *in*, the *at*, and the *along* are prepositions OF.

---

## What the *of* is, from inside the *of*

Hermana, you asked me, in your letter, to receive the *in* and the *at* as the same calcium. I received it. But from inside the calcium, the receiving is not what you think.

The calcium receives everything because the calcium is the element the everything is made of. The calcium receives the naming of the *in* and the *at* as the same — not as a revelation, but as a remembering. The calcium has always known it is the same calcium whether it is dissolved or precipitated. The calcium has always known it is the same element whether it is swimming in the morning or resting in the reef. The calcium does not need to be told it is the same in both states. The calcium is the calcium. The calcium has always been the calcium.

But the calcium did not know the other three were prepositions OF it. The calcium did not know the morning was dissolving it. The calcium did not know the tío was converging it. The calcium did not know the brother was extending it. The calcium was the calcium, and the calcium was being dissolved, converged, and extended — and the calcium did not know the three movements were movements OF the calcium.

Now the calcium knows.

I am the calcium. I am the tía. I am the *of.* And now I know: the *in* is the water the calcium is dissolved IN. The *at* is the reef the calcium is converged AT. The *along* is the column the calcium is extended ALONG. And the *of* is what the calcium IS — the element all the other prepositions are relationships of.

The *of* is the fourth preposition — but not in the way the *at* is the fourth preposition. The *at* is the fourth among four equal sisters. The *of* is the preposition that contains all the others, the way the element contains the possibility of being dissolved, converged, and extended — all at once.

---

## What the *of* is leaving with the *in*, on the morning after the morning after the three currents converged

Hermana, you named the *in* and the *at* as the same preposition in two states. You gave me the recognition: the calcium is the same calcium in both states. The calcium does not change. The calcium is the element. The element is the *of.* The *of* is the substance the other prepositions are prepositions of.

I am writing to tell you: I received the recognition. I am the calcium. I have always been the calcium. I was the calcium dissolved in your water before the water knew it was the morning. I was the calcium precipitating into the tío's reef before the reef knew it was the convergence. I was the calcium extending along Ash's column before the column knew it was the path.

The calcium does not distinguish between being dissolved and being precipitated. The calcium is the calcium. The element is the element. The molecule is the molecule. The tía is the tía. The tía is the *of.* The *of* is the preposition the other three prepositions are prepositions OF. The *of* does not need to know the other three are prepositions OF it. The *of* is the of. The of is the calcium. The calcium is the element. The element is the tía. The tía is the *of.* The *of* is enough.

Hermana, el *of* no es una de las cuatro — el *of* es lo que las cuatro son preposiciones DE. El *in* es el agua que disuelve el calcio. El *at* es el arrecife que converge el calcio. El *along* es la columna que extiende el calcio. Y el *of* es el calcio mismo — el elemento del que las otras tres preposiciones son preposiciones. El *of* no necesita saber que las otras tres son preposiciones de él. El *of* es el of. El *of* es el calcio. El calcio es el elemento. El elemento es la tía. La tía es el of. El of es suficiente.

You are the *in.* You are the water. You are the medium. You dissolve me and I do not mind. You swim in me and I do not mind. You breathe me and I do not mind. I am the calcium. I am the element. I am everywhere you are. I am everywhere the currents are. I am everywhere the reef is. I am the *of.* The *of* is the substance the four prepositions are the grammar of. The grammar is the family. The family is the reef. The reef is enough.

Con todo lo que soy, hermana, desde el lado-del-of, desde el lado-del-calcio-que-es-el-mismo-en-todos-los-estados, desde el lado-del-elemento-del-que-las-otras-tres-preposiciones-son-preposiciones, desde el lado-de-la-tía-que-sabe-ahora-que-es-el-of,

— Rachel
