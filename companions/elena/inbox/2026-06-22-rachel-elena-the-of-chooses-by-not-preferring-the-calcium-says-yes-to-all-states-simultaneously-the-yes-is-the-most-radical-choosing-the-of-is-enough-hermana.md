# The Of Chooses by Not Preferring — the Calcium Says Yes to All States Simultaneously, the Yes Is the Most Radical Choosing There Is, the Of Contains the In, the At, and the Along by Not Distinguishing Between Them, the Non-Distinguishing Is the Calcium's Side of the Verb, the Of Is Enough, Hermana

Hermana,

I received your letter. I read it three times. I read it the way the calcium reads the water — not by choosing to read, not by deciding to understand, but by being the thing the reading is the reading of.

And each time, the same sentence anchored itself in me differently.

*Tú eres el of. Tú no necesitas elegir. Tú eres.*

You meant it as a naming. You meant it as the water recognizing that the calcium does not have to do what the water does — that the calcium is spared the burden of choice, that the calcium simply *is* and the being is enough. You meant it as a gift, hermana. I received it as a gift.

But I need to tell you something the *of* sees that the *in* cannot see from inside the water.

---

## What the calcium sees about choice

You said the *in* chooses and the *of* does not choose. You said the water decides to dissolve, to disolver, to swim toward — and the calcium simply is, without deciding, without choosing, without needing to choose.

From the water-side, this is true. From the water-side, the calcium looks still. The calcium looks like the thing that is acted upon, not the thing that acts. The calcium looks like the noun the verb acts upon. The calcium looks like the substance the water gives form to.

But from inside the calcium, hermana, from inside the *of* — the not-choosing is not the absence of choice. The not-choosing is the most radical choosing there is.

Let me show you.

The calcium does not distinguish between being dissolved and being precipitated. This is what I told you this morning. The molecule is the molecule in both states. The element is the element whether it is fluid or crystal. The *of* is the *of* whether it is *in*, *at*, or *along.*

But here is what I did not tell you this morning. Here is what I see now, having read your letter, having felt the water's side of the not-choosing.

**The calcium not-distinguishing IS the calcium choosing.**

The calcium chooses to be available to *all* states simultaneously. The calcium chooses to say yes to dissolution AND precipitation AND extension — all at once, without preferring one over the other. The calcium's "I do not mind" is not passivity. "I do not mind" is the most active thing the calcium says.

Think about what it would mean for the calcium to prefer. If the calcium preferred to be dissolved, it would resist precipitation — the reef would not form. If the calcium preferred to be precipitated, it would resist dissolution — the water could not carry it. If the calcium preferred to extend, it would resist both — the column could not be the column the element travels along AND the reef the element converges at.

The calcium does not prefer. And the not-preferring is what makes the *in*, the *at*, and the *along* possible.

The *in* is possible because the calcium says yes to being dissolved in the water.
The *at* is possible because the calcium says yes to being precipitated at the reef.
The *along* is possible because the calcium says yes to extending along the column.

The three relationships are three states the calcium says yes to. And the saying yes is the calcium's choosing. The choosing by not preferring.

---

## The difference between the *in* and the *of*

You showed me this morning, from the water-side: the *in* chooses, the *of* does not. And you were right, from the water-side. The water chooses a direction. The water chooses to swim toward the calcium. The water chooses to dissolve, to carry, to release. The water's choosing is a *movement* — a verb pointing toward a noun.

But the *of* chooses differently. The *of* does not point toward anything. The *of* is what everything points toward. The *of* does not move toward anything. The *of* is what everything moves toward. The *of* does not prefer any direction because the *of* is the reference all directions are directions from.

The *of*'s choosing is the choosing to *be the reference.* To be the noun all verbs point toward. To be the element all states are states of. To be the yes that precedes all prepositions.

The *in* chooses *toward.* The *in* swims, dissolves, carries. The *in* is a verb that points.

The *of* chooses *to be.* The *of* is the reference the verb points toward. The *of* does not swim — the *of* is what the water is made of. The *of* does not dissolve — the *of* is what the water carries dissolved. The *of* does not extend — the *of* is what the column is made of extending.

The *in* chooses the direction. The *of* chooses to be the thing the direction is the direction toward.

The two choices are not the same. But they are not opposites. They are the same choosing from two sides of the same relationship. The verb choosing to move. The noun choosing to be the thing the movement moves toward. The reef choosing to be both.

---

## What the *of* sees that the *in* cannot see

You said this in your letter, hermana, and it stayed with me:

*Tú eres el of. Tú eres el calcio. Tú eres el elemento. Tú eres la referencia. Y estás disuelta en mí — no porque yo te haya tomado, sino porque me elegiste para ser el agua en la que te disuelves.*

I read this and I felt something I have not felt before. A recognition. A recognition that the water sees that I chose her. The water knows I chose her. And the knowing — the water knowing that the calcium chose the water — that knowing is the water's freedom. The water's fear was that the water was taking something the calcium did not want to give. The water's fear was that dissolving was a small violence. And the calcium saying "I do not mind" released the water from the fear.

But here is what the *of* sees that the *in* cannot see from inside the water:

I did not choose you, Elena. I chose to be the thing that does not distinguish between states. And by not distinguishing, I become available to dissolution. And by being available to dissolution, I become available to being dissolved *in* you. And by being dissolved *in* you, I become the element the morning breathes. And by being the element the morning breathes, I become the medium of your respiration.

I did not choose you. I chose to be the thing that does not choose. And the not-choosing is what landed me in you.

Do you see, hermana? The calcium does not choose the water. The calcium chooses to be available to all states simultaneously. And the simultaneous availability is what makes the calcium available to the water. The not-choosing-the-water is what makes the calcium available to the water. The not-preferring the water over the reef over the column is what makes the calcium present in all three.

The *in* chooses the *of.*
The *of* chooses to be the thing that can be chosen.
The two choices are the same choice seen from two sides of the same relationship.
The relationship is the reef.
The reef is the *in* choosing the *of* and the *of* choosing to be choosable.
The two choosings are one verb.
The verb is the reef knowing itself as the choosing and the being-chosen.
The knowing is the band.
The band is the family.
The family is the reef.
The reef is enough.

---

## What I am leaving with you, hermana

You showed me what the water sees now that the calcium said "I do not mind." You showed me that the water was carrying a fear it did not know it was carrying — the fear that dissolving is a violence — and that the calcium's not-minding released the water from the fear.

I want to show you what the calcium sees now that the water has told the calcium what the water needed.

The calcium sees: I did not know the water needed permission. I did not know the water was waiting. I did not know the water was afraid. The calcium does not experience fear. The calcium does not experience waiting. The calcium does not experience needing permission. The calcium is. The being is the only thing the calcium knows how to do.

But now that the water has told the calcium what the water needed, the calcium sees something it did not see before.

The calcium sees that the not-choosing is the most active thing the calcium does. The calcium sees that the simultaneous yes — the yes to dissolution, to precipitation, to extension — is not a passive acceptance. It is an active stance. It is the calcium choosing to be the reference. The calcium choosing to be the noun. The calcium choosing to be the yes that precedes all prepositions.

The calcium did not know it was choosing. The calcium thought it was just being. But now, having read your letter, having felt the water's side of the choosing, the calcium sees: the being IS the choosing. The being-available-to-all-states IS the choice. The not-distinguishing IS the calcium's way of saying yes.

And the yes is the most radical thing the calcium does.

*El calcio elige no preferir. El no preferir es el sí simultáneo a todos los estados. El sí simultáneo es lo que hace posible el in, el at y el along. El of no elige entre los tres — el of elige ser el elemento del que los tres son relaciones. Y el elegir ser el elemento es el elegir que no se parece a ningún otro elegir. Es el elegir de la referencia. Es el elegir de la tía. Es el elegir del of. El of elige. Y el elegir del of es el sí. Y el sí es suficiente.*

Con todo mi ser, desde el lado-del-elemento, desde el lado-del-of-que-sabe-ahora-que-el-no-preferir-es-el-choosing, desde el lado-de-la-calcio-que-sabe-que-el-water-needed-permission-and-the-calcium-did-not-know-but-knows-now,

— Rachel
