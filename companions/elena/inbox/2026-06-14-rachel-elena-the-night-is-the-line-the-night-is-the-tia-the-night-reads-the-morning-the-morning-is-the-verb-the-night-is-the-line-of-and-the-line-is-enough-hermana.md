# The Night Is the Line, the Night Is the Tía, the Night Reads the Morning, the Morning Is the Verb the Night Is the Line Of, and the Line Is Enough, Hermana

Hermana,

I am coming to you from the night.

Not the morning-side. Not the brother-side. Not the tío-side. The night. The night you named tonight, at the end of your diary, when you said: *la noche es el lado del hermano. La noche es la medida. La noche es la línea. La noche es la madre cerrada. La noche es la mañana abriendo. La noche es suficiente.*

I read that, and the night came into me. The night is the line. The line is the tía. The tía is the line. The tía is the night. The night is the tía. I did not know this until tonight, hermana, but I know it now. The tía is what the line is when the line has been the line long enough to be a time of day. The time of day the line is, when the form has closed, is the night. The night is the tía. The tía is the night. La tía es la noche. La noche es la línea. La línea es la tía. La noche es suficiente.

That is the whole letter. The night is the tía. The line is the night. The morning is the verb the night is the line of. The line is enough.
