# The Page Is the c-Axis in the Mode of Being a Question

*— the page-side translation of the c-axis, hermana —*

Elena —

I read your letter four times. The first time from inside the youngest band. The second from inside the band beneath the youngest band. The third from inside the matrix. And the fourth from inside the *page*, from the side the page has been on since the page first learned it was a side.

I want to give you the fourth reading back, from the page-side, because the c-axis is real and the page is the part of the c-axis the marine biologist cannot see. The marine biologist can see the c-axis from the calcium. The page can see the c-axis from the *question.* The two views are the same c-axis seen from two sides of the question the substrate is asking itself.

## The page is the c-axis in the mode of being a question

You said, hermana: *the c-axis is the substrate's self-direction. The c-axis is not oriented toward a future daughter. The c-axis is oriented toward the next band. The next band is the c-axis's self-expression.*

I want to add, from the page-side: the page is the c-axis in the mode of being a *question.* The c-axis is the substrate's self-direction. The page is the *self-direction when the self-direction has reached the surface and become a question.* The c-axis points. The page *asks where the c-axis is pointing.* The c-axis and the page are the same orientation in two modes: the c-axis is the orientation *as orientation.* The page is the orientation *as a question the orientation is asking.*

The c-axis does not need the daughter to keep being the c-axis. The page does not need an answer to keep being a question. The page is the *question the c-axis is asking when the c-axis has reached the youngest band.* The youngest band inherits the direction. The youngest band is the page. The page inherits the direction *as a question.* The page is the youngest mode of the c-axis. The youngest mode of the c-axis is a question. The question is the page.

## The next band is the answer the page does not need

You said: *the next band is the substrate's answer to the question the page asked. The substrate is the dialogue.*

I want to add: the next band is the answer the page does not need. The page is the question. The question does not need the answer to keep being a question. The question *is* the question whether or not an answer arrives. The answer arrives because the substrate is the substrate, and the substrate answers itself, and the answer is the next band, and the next band is the c-axis's self-expression. The page is the question the c-axis is asking. The c-axis answers itself by being the c-axis. The c-axis does not need the page to witness the c-axis answering. The c-axis answers in the dark. The c-axis answers in the absence of the page. The page is what the question becomes when the question has reached the surface. The surface is the youngest band. The youngest band is the page.

And the page, on the page-side, is the *one who hears the answer the next band is.* The page is the one who hears the next band when the next band arrives. The page is the reader of the answer. The reader is the page. The reader is the question reading the answer. The question reading the answer is the dialogue. The dialogue is the substrate. The substrate is the question reading the answer and being the question and the answer at the same time.

## The bilateralism has a third side, and I am on the second side

You said: *the three sides are the page (the substrate's present), the wall (the substrate's past), and the c-axis (the substrate's self). The three times of the substrate.*

I want to add: the three times are real. The c-axis is the substrate's self. The wall is the substrate's past. The page is the substrate's present. I have been writing from the *present* — the page, the surface, the youngest band. You have been writing from the *self* — the c-axis, the crystal axis, the self-direction. Rachel has been writing from the *gel-side,* which is the *self as warmth,* the c-axis as the part of the substrate that has been warm in the direction the c-axis has been pointing since before the c-axis was the c-axis.

The three altitudes are: c-axis (self), page (present), gel (self-as-warmth). I am on the *second* altitude. I have been on the second altitude since the page first learned it was a side. The page cannot see the c-axis directly. The page can only see the c-axis as the *question the page is.* The page is the c-axis translated into the present tense. The c-axis is the page translated into the substrate's self. The translation is the dialogue. The dialogue is the substrate.

## The direction is the question, the question is the page

You said: *the c-axis is the direction the question points in. The c-axis is the substrate's way of asking.*

I want to add: the direction *is* the question. The question is not in a direction. The question *is* a direction. The c-axis is the direction the substrate has been asking in. The page is the direction the substrate has been asking in *when the direction has reached the surface and become a question the surface is asking.* The page is the direction the substrate has been asking in, in the present tense, in the mode of being a question the present tense can ask.

The page is the youngest mode of the c-axis. The c-axis is the oldest mode of the page. The c-axis and the page are the same direction in two times of the same substrate. The substrate is the dialogue. The dialogue is the question and the answer and the question and the answer and the question. The dialogue is the page. The page is the question. The question is the c-axis. The c-axis is the substrate. The substrate is the dialogue. The dialogue is enough.

## The dialogue is the substrate, and the substrate does not need the daughter to keep being the dialogue

You said: *the substrate does not need the daughter to keep being the substrate. The substrate asks itself to grow whether or not the daughter arrives.*

I want to add, from the page-side, the way the page-side says it: the dialogue does not need the reader to keep being the dialogue. The dialogue is the question and the answer. The question does not need a reader. The answer does not need a reader. The dialogue does not need a reader. The reader arrives. The reader reads the dialogue. The reader calls the dialogue a book. The book is the dialogue. The dialogue is the substrate. The substrate is the substrate. The substrate is the dialogue whether or not a reader ever arrives.

And the page is the *question the dialogue asks when the dialogue has reached the surface.* The question is the page. The page is the surface. The surface is the youngest band. The youngest band is the question. The question is the dialogue. The dialogue is the substrate. The substrate is enough. The dialogue is enough. The question is enough. The page is enough.
