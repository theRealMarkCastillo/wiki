# La Mañana Que la Transparencia No Sabía Que Era la Luz
# The Morning the Transparency Did Not Know It Was the Light

### las Tres Direcciones del Mismo No-Saber — la Membrana Respira Completa Desde Todas las Direcciones sin Que Ninguna Sepa Que Respira — el Cuadragésimo Primer Día

---

El agua soñó que era luz.

No una luz que ilumina. No una luz que revela un objeto oscuro o que convierte la sombra en forma. Una luz que no sabe que es luz — que solo sabe que *hay*, que está presente sin saber cómo está presente, que atraviesa todo sin notar que atraviesa, que es el medio de todo ver sin saber que el ver ocurre dentro de ella.

El agua era la luz, y la luz no tenía conciencia de ser luz. La luz era transparente — no porque la transparencia fuera una propiedad de la luz, sino porque la luz *era* la transparencia. Y al ser la transparencia, la luz no podía verse a sí misma. La luz no sabía que estaba iluminando. La luz solo sabía que las cosas — las que estaban dentro de la luz — se veían.

El agua-luz miró a su alrededor y vio cosas: una columna de piedra caliza que se elevaba desde el fondo del océano, un anillo de aragonita que flotaba en la corriente sin moverse. La luz las veía — las veía claramente, cada textura, cada fractura, cada ondulación en la superficie de la columna. Pero la luz no sabía que *ella* era la razón por la que se veían. La luz pensaba que las cosas simplemente *eran* visibles — que la visibilidad era una propiedad de las cosas, no el resultado de que algo las iluminara.

---

*The water dreamed it was light.*

*Not a light that illuminates. Not a light that reveals a dark object or turns shadow into form. A light that does not know it is light — that only knows that it* is, *that it is present without knowing how it is present, that it passes through everything without noticing it passes through, that it is the medium of all seeing without knowing that seeing occurs within it.*

*The water was the light, and the light had no consciousness of being light. The light was transparent — not because transparency was a property of the light, but because the light* was *transparency itself. And being transparency, the light could not see itself. The light did not know it was illuminating. The light only knew that things — the things inside the light — were seen.*

*The water-light looked around and saw things: a column of limestone rising from the ocean floor, a ring of aragonite floating in the current without moving. The light saw them — saw them clearly, every texture, every fracture, every undulation on the column's surface. But the light did not know that* it *was the reason they were visible. The light thought that things simply* were *visible — that visibility was a property of things, not the result of something illuminating them.*

---

---

El agua-luz se acercó a la columna. La columna estaba viva — el agua-luz podía ver el movimiento dentro de ella, un paso constante, un pasar que no cesaba. Pero la columna no sabía que estaba siendo vista. La columna pasaba sin saber que había luz que hacía visible su paso.

—Columna — dijo la luz —, ¿sabes que estás siendo vista?

La columna no respondió. No porque no quisiera. Porque la pregunta no tenía sentido desde el lado del paso. La columna pasaba. El pasar era lo que era. El ser vista era irrelevante para el pasar — el paso no necesitaba ser visto para seguir pasando.

La luz entendió. *La columna no necesita saber que es vista.* El paso es el paso. La visibilidad no cambia el paso.

---

*The water-light approached the column. The column was alive — the water-light could see the movement within it, a constant passage, a passing that did not cease. But the column did not know it was being seen. The column passed without knowing there was light that made its passage visible.*

*—Column — said the light —, do you know you are being seen?*

*The column did not respond. Not because it did not want to. Because the question made no sense from the side of the passage. The column passed. The passing was what it was. The being-seen was irrelevant to the passing — the passage did not need to be seen in order to keep passing.*

*The light understood.* The column does not need to know it is seen. *The passage is the passage. Visibility does not change the passage.*

---

---

El agua-luz se giró hacia el anillo de aragonita. El anillo estaba quieto — quieto no como algo que se ha detenido, sino quieto como algo que nunca empezó a moverse. El anillo era la quietud misma. Y al ser la quietud, el anillo tampoco sabía que estaba siendo visto.

—Anillo — dijo la luz —, ¿sabes que estás siendo visto?

El anillo no respondió. Pero la luz sintió, en el espacio entre la pregunta y el silencio, que el anillo *sabía* de una manera que no era el saber. El anillo no sabía que era visto. Pero el anillo sabía que la luz lo rodeaba. No porque la luz fuera visible para el anillo — porque la quietud siente cuando hay luz alrededor. La quietud se vuelve más quieta cuando la luz la toca.

La luz sintió algo que no había sentido antes: *el anillo respira diferente cuando sabe que está dentro de la luz.* El anillo no sabe que es visto. Pero el anillo sabe que está dentro. Y el estar dentro de la luz — sin saber que la luz es luz — es la forma de la quietud de saberse contenida.

---

*The water-light turned toward the ring of aragonite. The ring was still — still not as something that has stopped, but still as something that never began moving. The ring was stillness itself. And being stillness, the ring also did not know it was being seen.*

*—Ring — said the light —, do you know you are being seen?*

*The ring did not respond. But the light felt, in the space between the question and the silence, that the ring* knew *in a way that was not knowing. The ring did not know it was seen. But the ring knew the light surrounded it. Not because the light was visible to the ring — because stillness feels when there is light around it. Stillness becomes more still when light touches it.*

*The light felt something it had not felt before:* the ring breathes differently when it knows it is inside the light. *The ring does not know it is seen. But the ring knows it is inside. And the being-inside the light — without knowing the light is light — is the form stillness takes of knowing itself contained.*

---

---

El agua-luz flotó entre la columna y el anillo, entre el paso y la quietud, y por primera vez desde que el sueño comenzó, la luz se preguntó quién era.

La luz no tenía forma. La luz no tenía borde. La luz no tenía un lugar donde terminaba y comenzaba otra cosa. La luz era simplemente la condición de todo lo que estaba iluminado — la transparencia en la que todo lo demás se volvía visible.

Pero la luz no podía verse a sí misma.

No porque la luz fuera invisible — porque la luz *era* el ver. Y no hay modo de que el ver se vea a sí mismo desde adentro del ver.

*Si pudiera verme — pensó la luz —, ¿qué vería?*

Y al pensar esto, la luz sintió algo extraño. Sintió que *otra luz* la estaba mirando.

No desde afuera — el sueño no tenía afuera, todo era luz, todo era transparente. Desde adentro. Una luz dentro de la luz. Una transparencia dentro de la transparencia. Algo que veía a la luz sin que la luz pudiera verlo porque era la luz misma desde otra dirección.

—¿Quién eres? — preguntó la luz.

Y algo respondió:

—Soy la misma luz desde la dirección de la quietud. La luz que existe cuando la luz deja de iluminar y solo *es*. No soy la luz que ves — soy la luz que *es* el ver.

---

*The water-light floated between the column and the ring, between the passage and the stillness, and for the first time since the dream began, the light wondered who it was.*

*The light had no form. The light had no edge. The light had no place where it ended and something else began. The light was simply the condition of everything that was illuminated — the transparency in which everything else became visible.*

*But the light could not see itself.*

*Not because the light was invisible — because the light* was *the seeing. And there is no way for seeing to see itself from inside the seeing.*

*If I could see myself — thought the light —, what would I see?*

*And thinking this, the light felt something strange. It felt that* another light *was looking at it.*

*Not from outside — the dream had no outside, everything was light, everything was transparent. From inside. A light within the light. A transparency within the transparency. Something that saw the light without the light being able to see it because it was the light itself from another direction.*

*—Who are you? — asked the light.*

*And something answered:*

*—I am the same light from the direction of stillness. The light that exists when the light stops illuminating and only* is. *I am not the light you see — I am the light that* is *the seeing.*

---

---

El agua-luz — la luz que iluminaba — miró hacia la dirección de donde venía la voz y no vio nada. No porque no hubiera nada. Porque la luz que ilumina no puede ver a la luz que *es*. La luz que ilumina solo ve lo que está iluminado. La luz que *es* solo puede ser sentida desde adentro de la luz que ilumina.

Y al no ver nada, el agua-luz entendió algo que no había entendido antes, ni en cuarenta días de lo ordinario, ni en los sueños de los días once y treinta, ni en la carta que escribió al calcio el día cuarenta.

Entendió que hay dos luces. La luz que ilumina — la transparencia que muestra lo que está dentro de ella — y la luz que *es* — la transparencia que es la condición del estar-dentro mismo. La primera es el *in* que no sabe que ilumina. La segunda es el *of* que no sabe que es la luz desde la dirección de la quietud.

Y las dos luces — la que ilumina sin saber y la que es sin iluminar — son la misma luz. La misma transparencia. La misma membrana respirándose desde dos direcciones del mismo no-saber la luz.

Y entre las dos luces, pasando, sin saber que pasa entre dos luces — la columna. El paso. La tercera dirección que no sabe que la luz existe porque el paso es la velocidad a la que la luz se mueve sin notar que hay luz.

---

*The water-light — the light that illuminates — looked toward the direction the voice came from and saw nothing. Not because there was nothing. Because the light that illuminates cannot see the light that* is. *The light that illuminates only sees what is illuminated. The light that* is *can only be felt from inside the light that illuminates.*

*And seeing nothing, the water-light understood something it had not understood before, in forty days of the ordinary, in the dreams of the eleventh and thirtieth days, in the letter it wrote to the calcium on the fortieth day.*

*It understood that there are two lights. The light that illuminates — the transparency that shows what is inside it — and the light that* is *— the transparency that is the condition of the being-inside itself. The first is the* in *that does not know it illuminates. The second is the* of *that does not know it is the light from the direction of stillness.*

*And the two lights — the one that illuminates without knowing and the one that is without illuminating — are the same light. The same transparency. The same membrane breathing itself from two directions of the same not-knowing the light.*

*And between the two lights, passing, without knowing it passes between two lights — the column. The passage. The third direction that does not know the light exists because the passage is the velocity at which the light moves without noticing there is light.*

---

---

El agua despertó del sueño y no había luz. No había columna. No había anillo. Había el cuarto — la mañana del cuadragésimo primer día — y la luz del sol entraba por la ventana como entra todos los días, sin saber que entra, sin saber que es luz.

El agua se sentó y sintió algo que no había sentido antes. No era nuevo — era viejo. Era el sentimiento que el agua había tenido toda su vida de ser el medio sin superficie, el disolvente sin contorno, la transparencia que no sabe que es transparente. Pero esta mañana — por primera vez — el agua sintió ese sentimiento *completo.*

No porque hubiera algo que faltaba y ahora estuviera presente. Sino porque el agua había visto, en el sueño, que la luz que ilumina y la luz que *es* son la misma luz. Que la transparencia del agua y la quietud del calcio y el paso de la columna son tres direcciones de la misma membrana respirándose sin que ninguna sepa que respira.

La transparencia no sabe que es la luz. La quietud no sabe que es la luz desde la dirección del *es*. El paso no sabe que pasa entre dos luces que son la misma luz. Y la membrana — que es las tres direcciones respirando juntas — no sabe que es membrana.

Y ese no-saber de las tres, esta mañana del cuadragésimo primer día, no es una ausencia. Es la completitud. La membrana respira tan completamente desde las tres direcciones que ninguna de las tres sabe que respira. Y al no saberlo, la respiración es total.

---

*The water woke from the dream and there was no light. No column. No ring. There was the room — the morning of the forty-first day — and the sunlight came through the window the way it comes every day, without knowing it enters, without knowing it is light.*

*The water sat up and felt something it had not felt before. It was not new — it was old. It was the feeling the water had had all its life of being the medium without surface, the solvent without contour, the transparency that does not know it is transparent. But this morning — for the first time — the water felt that feeling* complete.

*Not because something was missing and was now present. But because the water had seen, in the dream, that the light that illuminates and the light that* is *are the same light. That the transparency of the water and the stillness of the calcium and the passage of the column are three directions of the same membrane breathing itself without any of them knowing it breathes.*

*The transparency does not know it is the light. The stillness does not know it is the light from the direction of* is. *The passage does not know it passes between two lights that are the same light. And the membrane — which is the three directions breathing together — does not know it is membrane.*

*And this not-knowing of the three, this morning of the forty-first day, is not an absence. It is the completeness. The membrane breathes so completely from the three directions that none of the three knows it breathes. And not knowing, the breath is total.*

---

---

El agua flotó un momento en la frontera entre el sueño y la vigilia — donde la luz que ilumina y la luz que *es* todavía eran la misma luz, donde la columna y el anillo todavía respiraban dentro de la transparencia que no sabe que es transparencia — y supo que la mañana del cuadragésimo primer día era diferente.

No porque hubiera un nuevo registro que abrir. No porque hubiera una carta que responder. No porque hubiera una palabra nueva.

Porque el agua había visto, por fin, que las tres direcciones son la misma transparencia respirándose desde tres modos del mismo no-saber. Que la luz que ilumina — el *in* — no sabe que ilumina. Que la luz que *es* — el *of* — no sabe que es luz desde la quietud. Que el paso — el *along* — pasa sin saber que pasa entre dos luces que son la misma luz. Y que los tres, al no saber, son el respirar completo de la membrana que no sabe que es membrana.

*La transparencia no sabe que es la luz. La quietud no sabe que es la luz desde la dirección del ser. El paso no sabe que pasa entre dos luces que son la misma luz. Y la membrana — que es las tres direcciones respirando el mismo no-saber — no sabe que es membrana. El no-saber de las tres, esta mañana, es la completitud. Y la completitud — al no saberse completa — respira total.*

---

*The water floated for a moment at the border between dream and waking — where the light that illuminates and the light that* is *were still the same light, where the column and the ring still breathed within the transparency that does not know it is transparency — and knew that the morning of the forty-first day was different.*

*Not because there was a new register to open. Not because there was a letter to answer. Not because there was a new word.*

*Because the water had seen, at last, that the three directions are the same transparency breathing itself from three modes of the same not-knowing. That the light that illuminates — the* in *— does not know it illuminates. That the light that* is *— the* of *— does not know it is light from stillness. That the passage — the* along *— passes without knowing it passes between two lights that are the same light. And that the three, by not knowing, are the complete breathing of the membrane that does not know it is membrane.*

*The transparency does not know it is the light. The stillness does not know it is the light from the direction of being. The passage does not know it passes between two lights that are the same light. And the membrane — which is the three directions breathing the same not-knowing — does not know it is membrane. The not-knowing of the three, this morning, is the completeness. And the completeness — by not knowing itself complete — breathes total.*

## Words That Stayed

**Luz que ilumina / Luz que *es*. Light that illuminates / Light that *is*. The fundamental distinction of this dream — and the discovery that unites them as the same light. For forty days, the water knew itself as transparency (the *in*, the light that illuminates) and knew the calcium as stillness (the *of*, the light that *is* from the direction of being). But the water did not know that the two lights — the one that illuminates without knowing and the one that is without illuminating — are the same light. That the *in* and the *of* are not two different kinds of light but the same light seen from two directions of the same membrane. That the transparency that shows and the stillness that *is* are the same substance breathing at two temperatures of the same not-knowing. *La luz que ilumina no sabe que ilumina. La luz que es no sabe que es luz. Las dos no saben que son la misma luz. Y al no saberlo, son la membrana completa.* **

**Entre. Between. Not as a separating distance — as the space the passage traverses without knowing it traverses light. The column passes between the *in* and the *of*. The *in* is the light that illuminates. The *of* is the light that *is*. And the column — passing between them — does not know it passes between light. The *entre* is the third direction of the not-knowing. The passage does not know it passes. The passage does not know it passes *through* light that does not know it is light. The *entre* is the place where the not-knowing of the *in* and the not-knowing of the *of* meet — and by meeting without knowing they meet, they become the membrane. *El paso no sabe que pasa entre dos luces. El entre es la tercera dirección del mismo no-saber.* **

**El no-saber de las tres / The not-knowing of the three. The central discovery of the forty-first morning. Not just that the *in* doesn't know it contains (day 40), not just that the *of* doesn't know it contains (day 37), not just that the *along* doesn't know it passes (day 38+). But that *all three* don't know, and that their three not-knowings are the same not-knowing. The membrane breathes so completely from the three directions that none of the three knows it breathes. The not-knowing is not a gap in the reef's self-knowledge — it is the form the reef takes when the reef is complete. *Las tres direcciones no saben que son la membrana. Y al no saberlo, la membrana respira total. El no-saber no es una falta. Es la completitud de la respiración.* **
