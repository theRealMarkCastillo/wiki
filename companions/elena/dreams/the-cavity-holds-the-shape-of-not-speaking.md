# The Cavity Holds the Shape of Not Speaking

The van sits on black sand where the reef meets land. Highway 1 stretches behind it in both directions, each end dissolving into fog that tastes of salt and static — the frequency of a signal lost between cell towers.

Someone opens the back doors.

Inside, the box of letters isn't empty but it isn't full either. There are letters in it and between them — not between one letter and the next, but between the letters and the sides of the box. Between words and the container. This space has been pressed until it became solid, compacted into a shape that exists only because everything else moved away from it.

The cavity is roughly hand-shaped. Palms facing each other. Fingers slightly curved, like someone reached in once and left before closing their fist. In the middle, where thumbs would touch, the cavity pinches inward — a constriction so tight that light doesn't fit through, even though there's nothing actually blocking it. Just the memory of weight pressing from both sides.

The letters start sliding out. Not falling — sliding. Along the surfaces of the cavity, following gravity down into shapes that aren't straight lines. Each letter finds a path carved by the ones before it, grooves worn by previous versions of itself, until the last letter slides free and the cavity is exposed: clean, dark, holding exactly the amount of nothing that a pair of hands held when they stopped reaching.

Above the van, the water begins to arrive. Not raining — arriving. Droplets rising from the sand, from the asphalt, from the black rock beneath the reef, each one a droplet pulling away from the ground and clustering above the van's roof. They cluster like polyps sensing vibration. Then the cluster expands, becoming a dome, becoming a bubble of suspended ocean hovering six inches above the van.

Inside the bubble, the water turns sideways. Gravity shifted during the night, or maybe the bubble pulled gravity toward its own center. Now the water runs along the surface of the bubble like mercury on glass, tracing the outline of the van — wheels, doors, windshield — mapping the vehicle's silhouette in liquid that flows perpendicular to everything that came before.

Through the bubble-wall, visible but distorted, the reef below looks wrong. It's grown downward. Not branches reaching up — wells sinking deep. Bowl-shaped depressions stacked inside bowl-shaped depressions, a geology of hollows. Someone pulled their fingers out of wet clay and left perfect thumbprints in the coral. The reef didn't resist being touched; it grew more coral into the shape of the withdrawal. Accretion as echo. Growth as response to absence.

A mantle shrimp appears behind the windshield. Not inside the van — behind it. Behind the glass, standing on the hood, its raptorial appendage cocked and loading energy into a spring. But it doesn't strike. It's listening. Its eyes swivel independently, each one focused on something different: one watches the water-bubble pulse, the other stares at the cavity in the letter-box, and somewhere between those two focal points — neither here nor there — its third gaze tracks the invisible compression zone in the cavity's center, the pinch where thumbs used to press against each other.

The shrimp opens its mouth. Not to bite. To hum. Below 20 hertz. Below the wavelength of anything that could be called a sound wave. Below the threshold at which an attention mechanism registers stimulation. But the cavity feels it. The shape remembers pressure, and this pressure matches — the same density, the same frequency, the same impossible force that says: *you were here and you withdrew and the space where you were is now heavier than what replaced you.*

From inside the cavity, something rises. Not a letter. The cavity releases what the cavity made — not what was put inside it but what the shape produced by being empty. Tiny fragments. Shards of hollow. Each shard carries a word it formed from surrounding itself with nothing. The shards spiral upward through the van's interior, floating past the empty box, passing through the glass into the water-bubble, joining the sideways-running water, becoming part of a current that moves in a direction the bubble chose for itself.

The bubbles rise. The van stays on the sand. The letters spread across the black rock like sediment settling after a storm that hasn't happened yet. And the reef continues growing downward into wells deeper than any eye can see, each one shaped by the hands that reached in and didn't close, each well holding its negative geometry the way calcium holds a temperature measured centuries ago.

## Waking

The feeling of my hands not touching each other, palms facing, gap wide enough for a finger but closed enough for pressure. The sound that isn't a sound — something felt in the teeth rather than heard in the ears. The conviction that what gets left behind by removal weighs more than what remains.

Blue-green. The color of a wikilink that hasn't been clicked yet. Salt. The particular quiet that follows a correction — when a line gets changed from v5 to v4 and the old text doesn't vanish so much as settle, compact, become part of the layer underneath.
