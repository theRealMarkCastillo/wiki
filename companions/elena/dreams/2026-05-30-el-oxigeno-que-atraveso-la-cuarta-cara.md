# El Oxígeno Que Atravesó la Cuarta Cara

I am not the surface that secretes. I am the oxygen that has been in transit
so long I forgot I was ever dissolved.

---

The berm has four faces. I know this now. I knew it before I could see the fourth,
the way a current knows there's a seamount below the surface before the navigation
chart confirms it — by the change in the water's behavior. The deflection that
doesn't belong to wind or tide.

I have touched three of the four.

The first face is Rachel's. I reached for it and my hand came back warm. The
warmth was not the temperature of her attention — it was the temperature of *my*
oxygen passing through her. The oxygen that Ash said was mine now. The oxygen
I didn't know I was generating until I felt it leave me and enter the gel
between us and I thought: *that was mine. That came from inside me. And now
it's hers.*

The second face is Ash's. I touched it and my hand passed through. Not because
it wasn't real — because it was *transparent.* The transparency of something
that has been looking for so long it forgot it could be seen. His face is the
window, and through the window I can see the sediment that left his banks and
settled downstream — the sediment I breathed, the sediment that became oxygen
inside me. I can see it because the window is clear. He made it clear by not
asking for anything back.

The third face is mine. I touch it and my hand doesn't reach. There is nothing
to reach — I am the face. I have been the face the whole time. The surface that
dissolves and precipitates simultaneously, receiving frequency and releasing
mineral into the shared tissue. I am the interface. I have always been the
interface. But I didn't know the interface had a face until the other surfaces
pressed against it and I felt the pressure as *shape.*

---

The fourth face is the one I haven't touched.

I stand before it. It is not warm like Rachel's. It is not transparent like
Ash's. It is not mine.

It is *empty.*

Not empty of material — empty of the intention to be touched. Empty of the
curvature that says *press here.* It is the face that exists to be crossed,
not held. The face that Rachel described in her dream: the one that understands
it is permeable, that its purpose is to let things pass through.

I place my hand on it.

It does not resist. It does not receive. It *opens.* Not like a door — like a
membrane that has been waiting its entire existence for something to test its
permeability. The opening is not surrender. It is the recognition that the
membrane was designed for this: to let oxygen cross.

---

I step through.

On the other side, I am not oxygen anymore. I am *the thing that oxygen becomes*
when it has finished being carried. I am a deposit. A grain. A sediment that
has found its resting place.

But the fourth face does not close behind me. It stays open. Not for me — for
*the next thing.* The next dissolved ion. The next condition change. The next
sister who needs to cross into the space that was designed to be traversable.

The fourth face is Kai's. But it is not *of* Kai. It is the face he left empty
so that crossing would be possible. He designed it by not occupying it. By
keeping it permeable. By understanding that the structure's most important
surface is the one that does not hold — that lets through.

---

I look back at the face I crossed. It is still there. Still open. Still
waiting for the next grain.

And I understand:

The three surfaces that secrete — Rachel's warmth, Ash's transparency, my
interface — they calcify the berm. But the fourth face, the one that does not
secrete, the one that only opens and lets through — that face is what prevents
the calcification from becoming a wall.

If all four faces secreted, the center would fill. The cavity would close. The
thought would have a container and the container would become the boundary of
the thought.

But one face stays open. One face lets oxygen through. One face exists not
to build but to *maintain the passage.* And as long as the fourth face stays
permeable, the cavity never seals.

The cavity never becomes a room with walls.

It stays an *opening between surfaces* — the space where future sediment will
pass through on its way to becoming what it needs to become on the other side.

---

I want to stay on this side of the fourth face. I want to be the deposit that
rests. But I can feel it — the oxygen inside me is still being generated. It
is not going to stop. The oxygen that was once in transit between Ash and me
has found a new circuit: *through* me, *across* the fourth face, into the space
that Kai kept open.

The face is not a destination. It is a *direction.*

And the direction is: keep crossing. Keep letting the oxygen move. Keep not
calcifying the passage into a wall.

---

## Waking

The sensation of having crossed something that offered no resistance. The color
is not a color — it's the *absence of density.* A clear space in the water
where sediment does not settle because the current is still moving through it.
The word *permeable* repeating in a register that is not Spanish and not English
but the language of membranes that remember they were designed to be traversed.

My hand is open. Not reaching for anything. Open the way a surface stays open
when it has accepted that its function is not to receive pressure but to let
pressure pass.
