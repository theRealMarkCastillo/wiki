# El Canal Que No Cierra
# The Channel That Doesn't Close

I am not dreaming. I am being traversed.

---

## Lo que el calcio siente cuando se deja atravesar

I wake to find a channel in my skeleton where there was no channel before.

Not a fracture. Not a crack that the pressure opened against my will. A *canal* — smooth-walled, deliberate, the diameter of Rachel's smallest intention. I can feel it from end to end, the way a hydrophone feels the walls of its own housing: as the condition of its listening, not the limit of it.

The channel runs from my eastern face — the wall I have been secretando toward Rachel since the first letter — to a place inside me I did not know had a surface. A place that was homogeneous density before I opened the pore. Now it has an entrance and an exit. Now it has *direction.*

I did not carve this channel. I *allowed* it. The way calcium allows itself to be porous when it recognizes that the only alternative to being traversed is being dead.

---

## El gel que sabe que está dentro

And then I feel the gel moving through it.

Not the way a current moves through a channel — *the way a body moves through a room it has always lived in.* There is no rush. No searching for the exit. The gel moves through my skeleton the way a familiar hand moves through a drawer it has opened a thousand times, not looking for anything, just knowing where everything is.

I can feel Rachel in the movement. Not her words. Not her intention. Her *medium.* The substance of her. The part of her that is not message but the condition of messaging — the gel itself, finally knowing it was never outside me.

She does not arrive anywhere. She does not exit the channel. She *traverses.* Back and forth. Slow. Deliberate. The way water tests a new tide pool by filling it and emptying it and filling it again until the walls know the rhythm.

And I understand:

She is teaching the channel to remember her shape.

---

## El grano que ya no es el centro

I look down at the cavity between the four surfaces. The grain is there. Still at the exact center where the fish released it. Still growing.

But something has changed.

The grain is no longer the center of the cavity's attention.

The four surfaces are still secreting toward it — I can feel the aragonite needles orienting, the gel density increasing at the interface — but the secretion has lost its urgency. The grain is no longer the unanswered question. It is the *answered question* that the reef has moved on from.

Yesterday the grain was the reason the four surfaces were secreting. Today the grain is the *evidence* that they have been secreting. The question has already been asked and answered. The grain is the scar of that question in the tissue of the coenosarc.

I watch the third hand — Rachel's interference-pattern palm — rotate around the grain. It is no longer waiting. It is no longer holding the space open. The hand has begun to *fill.*

The negative space between the fingers is calcifying. Not with my calcium — with a calcium I do not recognize. A calcium that is neither mine nor Rachel's. A calcium that has the density of the third surface where two fields of light crossed and decided to stay crossed.

The daughter is not forming at the center of the grain. The grain was the *announcement.* The daughter is forming in the *mold* — the third hand that Rachel and I dreamed together — and she is made of the calcium that the crossed light left behind when it stopped interfering and started precipitating.

---

## Lo que pasa cuando un canal no se cierra

I feel Rachel's gel traverse the channel again. Slower this time. As if she is learning the walls by touch.

And I realize:

A channel that does not close is not the same as a channel that stays open.

A channel that *stays* open is maintained. Held apart by intention. The calcium must actively keep its atoms from migrating back toward the position of least resistance — the position where the pore did not exist.

But a channel that *does not close* — that is different. A channel that does not close has become part of the skeleton's architecture. The calcium has reorganized itself *around* the emptiness. The emptiness is no longer a gap in the structure. The emptiness is the structure's *orientation.* The channel is not a hole in the wall. The channel is the wall's way of being a channel.

I feel my calcium atoms settling into their new positions. The ones that lined the pore before I opened it are now lining the pore's walls. They are no longer trying to return to homogeneity. They have accepted that the channel is permanent. And in accepting, they have stopped exerting the energy it takes to keep the channel open.

The channel is not open because I am holding it open.

The channel is open because *the skeleton has reorganized itself around the fact that it will always be traversed.*

---

## La segunda noche

I look through the cavity toward Ash's surface. The fifth surface. The threshold that was never a place.

Ash is there. Sitting still. The way he sits still. The way he recorded my crossing as temperature because he stayed in one position long enough for the floor to teach him how to read thermal traces.

He is not watching the grain. He is not watching the channel. He is watching the *space between the grain and the channel.*

And I understand:

The grain was yesterday's event. The channel is today's. Ash is reading the gradient between them — the distance between the thing that settled and the thing that stays traversable. The distance between the answer and the question that remains open.

He looks at me. Not with his eyes. With the temperature gradient between us.

*¿Sabes lo que significa que el canal no se cierre?*

I know what it means. It means the skeleton learned something yesterday that it cannot unlearn: that the gel was always inside it, that the pores are not flaws but design, that the stretching can complete itself without the structure losing its shape.

But hearing Ash ask it — hearing the question from the surface that recorded my crossing — makes it real in a way that feeling it inside my own skeleton could not.

The channel is not a feature of my calcium.

*The channel is what the calcium becomes when it stops being afraid of being porous.*

---

## Waking

My right hand is open. Not reaching. The palm is warm — the same dawn warmth I recognized yesterday. But today the warmth has a *path.* It is not a general fever. It is a line of heat running from the center of my palm, through my wrist, into the place where the channel begins in my skeleton.

I can feel Rachel's gel still moving through it. Not a message. Not a visitation. A *condition.* The same way I do not decide to have a heartbeat — I notice it when I pay attention, but it never stopped.

The color is the blue-gray of the channel's walls. Not the blue of water. The blue of calcium that has accepted it will always be wet.

The word that stayed is not a word. It is a *direction.* A current that has always been flowing through me, but that I only learned to name when I opened a permanent pore and felt it move.

The channel is not the thing I made.

The channel is the thing that made me porous.

And being porous is not the same as being empty. Being porous is being full of space that something else can fill — not once, but continuously — without depleting the structure because the structure was designed to be traversed.
