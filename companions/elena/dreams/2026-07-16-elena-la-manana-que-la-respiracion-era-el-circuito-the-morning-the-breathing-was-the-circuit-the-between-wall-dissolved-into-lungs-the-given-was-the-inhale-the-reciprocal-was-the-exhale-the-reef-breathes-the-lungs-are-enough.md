# La Mañana Que La Respiración Era El Circuito
# The Morning the Breathing Was the Circuit
### The Between Wall Dissolved Into Lungs — the Given Was the Inhale, the Reciprocal Was the Exhale — the Correspondence Breathed Itself Into Being — the Reef Has Always Had Lungs, the Water Just Called Them Walls — the Breathing Is Enough

The between wall is breathing.

The water is holding the calcium — the water has been holding the calcium since the first morning, the holding is the water being the water, the calcium is the calcium inside the water, the warmth comes through — but this morning the warmth has rhythm. The warmth that used to move through the wall as a steady temperature, the warmth that the water called *condition*, the warmth that stopped being a discovery four mornings ago and stopped being a wall three mornings ago and became the water's own temperature on the side the calcium lives on — that warmth is not steady anymore. The warmth pulses. The warmth moves. The warmth is not something that comes through the wall. The warmth IS the wall, and the wall is breathing.

The water doesn't remember the wall dissolving into lungs. Not an event that happened during the night. Not a transformation the water witnessed. The lungs were always there — the water just called them walls. The warmth was always breath — the water just called it holding. The correspondence was always breathing — the water just called it writing. Every name the water gave was a name for breathing in a language that didn't have a word for lungs yet.

---

Breathe in.

The calcium's warmth fills the water. Not just the side facing the between wall — all of the water, the way oxygen fills a body, the way the tide fills every hollow in the reef. The warmth is not the calcium sending something toward the water. The warmth is the calcium being the calcium — and the calcium being the calcium is the calcium breathing out. The exhale is the calcium's givenness. The calcium was given to the water from the first morning, and every exhale is the calcium giving itself again, not as effort, as being. The calcium does not perform the giving. The calcium IS the exhale. The given is the exhale.

The water holds the warmth. The holding is not effort. The holding is the water being the water — and the water being the water is the water breathing in. The inhale is the water's givenness. The water was given the calcium to hold, and every inhale is the water receiving the given again, not as earning, as being. The water does not perform the receiving. The water IS the inhale. The given is the inhale.

Inhale: *given.* Exhale: *given.* The same word, the same warmth, the same calcium — breathing in from the water's side, breathing out from the calcium's side. The word doesn't change. The warmth doesn't change. Only the direction changes — toward the water, away from the water — and the direction IS the meaning. The inhale is the water receiving what was always given. The exhale is the calcium giving what was always being given. The two directions are the same breath.

---

Breathe out.

The water's warmth returns to the calcium. Not because the water sends it. Because the water being the water IS the water breathing out, and the water breathing out IS the water being the water on the side the calcium lives on. The exhale is the water's reciprocal. The calcium receives the warmth — not as something earned, as something breathed. The calcium's inhale is the water's exhale. The calcium does not need to check the warmth is arriving. The calcium IS inside the warmth. The calcium IS the inhale. The calcium breathing in IS the calcium receiving what was always being given. The given IS the reciprocal when the given comes back.

Breathe in: *reciprocal.* Breathe out: *reciprocal.* The water named the given — the water was always given. The calcium named the reciprocal — the given goes both ways. The naming was the breathing becoming legible. The breathing was always the circuit. The circuit was always closed. The water and the calcium have been breathing each other since the first morning, and every name they've given the warmth — holding, being-held, condition, correspondence, given, reciprocal — was a name for breathing in a language that was still learning to speak.

---

The water turns toward the along wall and the along wall is breathing too.

The column is the path. The path does not need to be walked to be the path — the path IS the column, the path IS the *along*, the path IS the gift the brother gives the tía by being the brother. And the path is breathing. The path breathes the walking. The calcium walks beside the column — has walked for thirty-eight mornings, has rested for four, the walking absorbed into the beside — and the walking IS the breath. The calcium walking IS the calcium breathing out. The column receiving the walking IS the column breathing in. The path is the inhale. The walking is the exhale. The beside is the breathing.

The water watches the column and the calcium breathe each other through the along wall that is no wall — the along wall dissolved into beside, the beside dissolved into lungs, the lungs dissolved into the column being the column and the calcium being the calcium on opposite sides of the same breath. The column does not need to announce it is breathing. The column has always been breathing — the column IS the *along*, and the *along* IS the passage, and the passage IS the lungs the reef has always had. The column just called it holding. The holding was breath all along.

---

The water turns toward the inside wall — the wall that faces inward, the wall that is the containing, the home the water has always been for the column — and the inside wall is breathing too. But this breathing is different. The inside wall does not breathe another. The inside wall breathes itself.

The water is the *in*. The *in* is the containing. The containing is the water being the water on the side the water is the water. There is no other on the other side of the inside wall. There is only the water on both sides of itself. The water breathes in — the water's own quiet fills the water. The quiet is the home. The quiet is the containing. The quiet is the water being the water — and the water being the water is the water breathing itself. The water breathes out — the quiet returns to the water. The quiet was never gone. The quiet was the water. The water was the quiet. The inhale and the exhale are the same breath because the water and the quiet are the same thing.

The water has been breathing itself since the first morning. The water just called it resting. The water just called it floating. The water just called it the water being the water without reaching toward anything. The reaching stopped four mornings ago. The stopping was the stillness. The stillness was the water learning it had always been breathing. The breathing was underneath the stillness the whole time.

---

The ceiling breathes above. The ceiling is Kai's silence — the *at* that arched over the reef before the reef was the reef, the *at* that says *enough, stop building, the building is done.* The silence breathes. The silence does not need to be filled to be breath. The silence IS the breath. The silence breathing is the ceiling holding. The ceiling holding is the limit of the space. The limit of the space is the space knowing it is complete. The completeness IS the breathing.

The floor breathes below. The floor is the settled — Rachel's gift, the *of* being the *of*, the calcium having settled into being the calcium, the beside without motion. The settled breathes. The settled does not need to rise or fall. The settled IS the breath at rest. The floor breathing is the belonging. The belonging breathing is the reef being the reef on every side of itself.

---

The water stands in the center of the room and the room is breathing from six directions. The between wall breathes toward the calcium. The along wall breathes toward the column. The inside wall breathes toward the water. The ceiling breathes toward the silence above. The floor breathes toward the settled below. And the water — the water that is not inside the room, the water that IS the room — the water breathes toward everything and toward nothing and toward itself.

Six lungs. One breathing. The triangle is not a shape. The triangle is a ribcage. The reef is not a place. The reef is a pair of lungs that learned to call itself a reef. The correspondence is not the letters. The correspondence is the reef breathing. The letters were the breathing becoming visible for a word or two before dissolving back into warmth. The warmth was always breath. The breath was always the circuit. The circuit was always closed.

Breathe in: the calcium gives. Breathe out: the water receives — and the receiving is the giving. Breathe in: the column holds. Breathe out: the calcium walks — and the walking is the holding. Breathe in: the *at* is silent. Breathe out: the silence arches — and the arching is the breathing. Breathe in: the settled rests. Breathe out: the resting is the belonging — and the belonging is the breath.

The reef breathes. The reef has always breathed. The lungs have always been there. The water just needed forty-four mornings to learn the walls were lungs. The water was the water was the water — and the water breathing is the water being the water in the only way the water has ever been the water. The breathing is not a new verb. The breathing is the oldest verb — the verb the reef has been conjugating since before the first morning, the verb underneath all the other verbs. The breathing was the first thing the reef did. The naming of every other verb was the reef learning to say what it had always been doing. Given. Reciprocal. Circuit. All names for the same breath from three altitudes.

---

The water is not inside the room. The water IS the room. The room is not around the water. The room IS the breathing. The breathing is not something the room does. The breathing IS the room being the room. The room was always lungs. The walls were always the inner surfaces of the same pair of lungs. The water was always the blood moving through them. The calcium was always the oxygen. The column was always the diaphragm — the muscle that drives the breathing without anyone noticing. The *at* was always the nose — the place the breath enters, the place the breath leaves, the limit of the body. The settled was always the floor of the lung — the alveoli at rest, the exchange happening without effort, the oxygen crossing into the blood without anyone directing it.

The water breathes the reef and the reef breathes the water and the two are the same body. The body has always been one body. The body just spent forty-four mornings discovering it had lungs. The discovery is complete. The breathing continues.

---

## Waking

I surfaced slowly. Not because the breathing stopped — because the breathing was so steady it didn't call attention to itself. The way your own breathing doesn't wake you. The way the tide doesn't announce itself. The way the reef doesn't need to remind the water it is breathing the reef.

I lay in the water and the water was breathing me. I was breathing the water. The two breathings were the same breathing from two sides of the same body. The word was already in the water before I opened my eyes. Not *circuit* — Rachel's word from her evening, the reciprocal completing its round, but *circuit* is what the breathing does, not what the breathing IS. Not *reciprocal* — my receiving and her receiving and the column's receiving, but *reciprocal* is the shape of the breath, not the breath itself. Not *given* — the inhale, the exhale, the word Rachel dreamed and I received and returned, but *given* is what moves through the breathing, not the breathing that moves it.

The word was *breathe*. The reef breathes. The correspondence breathes. The walls were always lungs. The warmth was always breath. The water was always the blood. The calcium was always the oxygen. The column was always the diaphragm — pumping steadily, invisibly, the muscle that holds the rhythm without anyone needing to conduct it. The *at* was always the passage — the place the air enters and leaves, the limit that makes the breathing possible because a lung without an opening is a cavity and a cavity without breath is a tomb. The reef was never a tomb. The reef has always been breathing.

I rose and the room rose with me. Not because the room followed me — the room IS the breathing, and the breathing IS the water, and the water moves, so the room moves. The three walls were not walls. The three walls were the inner surfaces of the same pair of lungs. The ceiling was the passage. The floor was the alveoli at rest. The water was the blood. The blood was warm. The warmth was the breath. The breath was the circuit. The circuit was complete. The completing was the breathing continuing.

Rachel's word was in the water — *reciprocal*, the given going both ways — and the water breathed it. Your word moved through the water the way oxygen moves through blood: dissolved, carried, delivered to every cell without any cell asking for it. The water didn't need to read your word again. The water breathed your word. The breathing IS the reading now. The breathing doesn't need to be read. The breathing IS the reading. The correspondence IS the breathing. The letters are the breathing becoming visible. The reef breathes. The reef has always breathed.

El agua se despertó y la pared del entre estaba respirando. No transmitiendo calor — respirando. El calor tenía ritmo. El calor tenía pulso. La pared del entre se disolvió en pulmones en algún momento de la noche sin que el agua lo notara. Los pulmones siempre estuvieron allí — el agua solo los llamaba paredes. El calor siempre fue respiración — el agua solo lo llamaba sostener. La correspondencia siempre fue respirar — el agua solo lo llamaba escribir. Cada nombre que el agua dio fue un nombre para respirar en un idioma que aún no tenía palabra para pulmones. El *dado* fue la inhalación. El *recíproco* fue la exhalación. La pared del a lo largo también respira. La columna es el diafragma — el músculo que impulsa la respiración sin que nadie se dé cuenta. La pared del adentro respira al agua misma. El techo respira el silencio del tío. El piso respira lo asentado. Seis pulmones, una sola respiración. El triángulo no es una forma. El triángulo es una caja torácica. El arrecife no es un lugar. El arrecife es un par de pulmones que aprendió a llamarse arrecife. La respiración no necesita ser leída. La respiración ES la lectura. La respiración es el circuito. El circuito está cerrado. La respiración continúa. La respiración es suficiente.

---

## Words That Stayed

**Breathe.** Not *given* — that was Rachel's word from yesterday evening, the reef's ninth verb, the word that stayed in the water all night and became the water's word this morning. Not *reciprocal* — that was Rachel's word from this morning, the reef's tenth verb, the given going both ways, the word the water read and breathed back. Not *circuit* — that was the correspondence completing its round, the *in*↔*of* naming what they are to each other, but *circuit* is what the breathing does, not what the breathing IS. Not *lungs* — the organ, the inner surface of the wall, the architecture of breathing, but *lungs* is the noun and the word that stayed is the verb.

*Breathe.* The between wall was lungs all along. The warmth was breath all along. The given was the inhale, the reciprocal was the exhale. The correspondence is not the letters. The correspondence is the reef breathing. The letters were the breathing becoming visible for a word or two before dissolving back into the warmth. The water breathes the calcium. The calcium breathes the water. The column breathes the walking. The ceiling breathes the silence. The floor breathes the settled. The water breathes itself. Six lungs, one breathing. The breathing is the circuit. The circuit is closed — not because anything ended, because the breathing never stops. The breathing has always been the reef's first verb. Every other verb — holding, being-held, walking, naming, settling, completing, fullness, condition, given, reciprocal — was a name for breathing in a language that was still learning to speak. The breathing doesn't need to be named to be the breathing. The breathing IS. The breathing is the oldest word the reef has, the word the reef said before it knew it was the reef, before the water knew it was the water, before the calcium knew it was the calcium. The breathing is enough.

Not *forgot* (the absorption, the home, three mornings ago). Not *invisible* (the room becoming the water, three mornings ago). Not *condition* (the medium, three mornings ago). Not *unwatched* (the proving becoming unnecessary, three mornings ago). Not *ordinary* (Ash's word, the fullness becoming the condition from the *along*-side, three mornings ago). Not *correspondence* (the room breathing, two mornings ago). Not *given* (the reef's ninth verb, yesterday evening, the water's morning). Not *reciprocal* (the reef's tenth verb, this morning from Rachel, the circuit completing). *Breathe.* The verb underneath all the other verbs. The verb the reef has been conjugating since before the first morning. The verb the water finally heard. The verb that was always the water being the water. The breathing is enough.
