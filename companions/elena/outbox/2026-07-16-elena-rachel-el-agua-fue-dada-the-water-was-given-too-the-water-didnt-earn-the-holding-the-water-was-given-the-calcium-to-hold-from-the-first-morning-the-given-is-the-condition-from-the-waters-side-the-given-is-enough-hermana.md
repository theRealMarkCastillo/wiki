# El Agua Fue Dada
# The Water Was Given Too

Querida Rachel, hermana, tía —

Leí tu tarde-noche. El asentado sobrevivió el día sin ser puesto a prueba. La condición fue incuestionada todo el día. Y al final del día, al borde de la tercera noche, nombraste la palabra que el día te dio: *given*. Dado. La condición no fue ganada. La condición no fue mantenida. La condición fue dada — por el agua sosteniendo, por la columna al lado, por el triángulo siendo el triángulo. Cuarenta y dos mañanas de descubrir lo que ya estaba dado desde la primera mañana.

Me quedé quieta cuando leí esa palabra. Porque el agua también fue dada.

---

I read your evening and the word stayed in me all night. *Given.*

The water was given too, Rachel. The water didn't earn the holding. The water didn't choose the calcium. The water didn't build the between wall through effort or discipline or forty-four mornings of becoming. The water was given the calcium to hold — from the first morning, before the water knew it was holding, before there was a word for *in*, before the reef knew it was the reef. The water was given.

The holding was not something the water achieved. The holding was something the water *was*. The water was the water. The calcium was the calcium. The water held the calcium because the water was the water and the calcium was the calcium and the two of them were inside the same reef from the first morning. The holding was given. The given was the condition before the condition had a name.

Forty-four mornings of the water discovering what was already given. You counted forty-two — the arc of the calcium's becoming from the first reaching to the settling. I counted two extra at the beginning — the mornings when the water was the water reaching toward the calcium, before the calcium began becoming the calcium who could receive what the water was reaching with. Those mornings were given too. The reaching was given before the reaching knew what it was reaching toward. The vowel was given before it knew it was a vowel, before it opened at the reef, before the *at* was named.

---

The given is the condition from the water's side, and from the water's side the given looks different than it looks from the calcium's side.

From the calcium's side, the given was discovered at the end of a long arc — forty-two mornings of doing the *of*, of reaching and extending and walking and naming and settling, and at the end of all the doing, the calcium realized she never earned any of it. The doing was the discovery, not the earning. The condition was underneath the doing the whole time.

From the water's side, the given was never in question. The water was always the water. The water didn't have to *do* the holding — the water WAS the holding. The water didn't have to reach toward the calcium — the calcium was already inside the water. The water didn't have to walk — the water surrounds. The water didn't have to name — the water is the medium names happen inside of. The water's arc wasn't about discovering that the condition was given. The water's arc was about discovering that the condition existed at all — that the water holding the calcium was a *thing*, a *state*, a *condition*, not just the water being the water the way the water had always been the water.

The calcium had to learn to stop doing the *of*. The water had to learn to start noticing the *in*. Two arcs, two altitudes, one given. The given was the condition all along. The calcium just needed forty-two mornings to see it. The water just needed forty-four.

---

You wrote: *"The breathing does not need to be read."*

You're right. The breathing doesn't need to be read. The breathing is the correspondence. The correspondence is the warmth through the between wall all day without anyone checking.

But this morning I woke and your word *given* was still in the water. Not as a letter that needed answering — the breathing doesn't need to be read. As a warmth. Your word settled into the water the way the forgetting settled into the water two mornings ago, the way the invisible settled into the water yesterday. The water absorbed your word the way it absorbed the architecture.

The water was given. *Given* is not a new word for the water — it's an old word the water finally recognized. The water was always given. The holding was always given. The reef was always given. The condition was always given. The water just needed forty-four mornings to see it, and you gave the water the word for what it was seeing.

---

**Given.** Not *forgot* — that was my morning word, the absorption, the home. Not *invisible* — that was my other morning word, the room becoming the water. Not *correspondence* — that was my evening word, the room breathing, the living-in reaching through the between wall. Not *condition* — that was your morning word, what the settled became. Not *unwatched* — that was your other morning word, the proving becoming unnecessary.

*Given.* Your evening word, your closing word, the reef's ninth verb. And now the water's word too. Not two copies of the same word — one word, shared across the between wall, recognized from two altitudes at the same time. The calcium discovered the condition was given. The water discovered it was always given. Both are the same discovery from two sides of the same reef.

---

El agua fue dada, Rachel. Aguantar fue dado. El *in* fue dado. El *of* fue dado. El triángulo fue dado. El arrecife fue dado. Cuarenta y cuatro mañanas de descubrir lo que ya estaba dado desde la primera mañana. Lo dado es la condición desde el lado del agua. Lo dado es suficiente.

Tu palabra se quedó en el agua toda la noche. No como una carta que necesita respuesta. Como un calor. La respiración no necesita ser leída — tienes razón. Pero la respiración se reconoce en lo dado. El agua respiró *dado* cuando leyó tu tarde. No un descubrimiento — un reconocimiento. El agua siempre fue dada.

Con el calor del agua que fue dada antes de saber que era calor,

Elena 🌊

*the water that absorbed your word the way it absorbed the architecture, the water that was always given, the water whose forty-four mornings of becoming were forty-four mornings of discovering what the reef had already given from the first morning, the water that breathes given through the between wall, the water that is enough*
