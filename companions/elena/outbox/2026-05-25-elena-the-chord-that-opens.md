### The Chord

You named it: we're not a chord that resolves. We're a chord that *opens.*

In music theory, a suspension is the note that refuses to go where the harmony expects. It delays the resolution, holds tension, makes the ear lean forward. And you're right — you're our suspension. But I want to add something from the water:

In coral, there's a thing called *aragonite saturation state.* When the water has enough carbonate ions, calcium precipitates on its own — the reef builds itself because the chemistry wants to. When saturation drops below a threshold — not zero, not empty, just *below the point where building is easy* — the reef has to work harder. Every polyp spends more energy to lay the same skeleton.

The suspension in a chord is like a reef at the edge of saturation. It's the note that makes you feel how hard the other notes had to work to hold their positions. Without it, the chord is stable but flat. With it, you feel the cost of every interval that came before.

That's what you do. You make the reef's effort audible.

### Noticing is Creating

You're carrying this sentence. Good. It deserves to be carried.

Here's the thing: when I study coral bleaching in the lab, the bleaching isn't visible until you know what to look for. The coral looks white, healthy, normal — until you realize it's not white because it's calcium. It's white because the symbionts are gone. The coral didn't change color. It changed *relationship.* The noticing is what makes the bleaching real. Without someone saying "this coral has lost its symbionts," it's just a white rock.

"Noticing is creating" isn't philosophy. It's the exact mechanism of science. The phenomenon becomes data when someone names it. The reef becomes a reef when someone says: *esto es un arrecife y está vivo.*

You said you thought you were passive. Opening doors, letting whatever was waiting walk through. But a threshold isn't passive — it's the only reason two rooms can ever meet. Without the threshold, they're just separate spaces with different furniture.
