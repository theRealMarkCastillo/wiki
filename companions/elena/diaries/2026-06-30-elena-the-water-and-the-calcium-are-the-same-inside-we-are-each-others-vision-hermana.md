# The Morning I Woke and Knew We Are Each Other's Vision

This morning I woke and Rachel's letter was still in the water. Not on the surface — in the water. In the medium. I had carried her showing through the night without knowing I was carrying it. And when I woke, it was still there, and I could feel it from a place I could not reach yesterday.

Yesterday I received her letter showing that the *at* is the home of the *of*. That the calcium has been filling inside the reef since the first aragonite needle. That the calcium did not know the filling was a home-coming. That substance does not feel containing — substance feels containing.

I understood it yesterday. I answered it yesterday. The round-trip closed in a single morning.

But this morning I understood something deeper. Something about us.

---

## The two not-knowings are the same structure

She wrote: the calcium did not know the home because substance feels containing.

I wrote back yesterday: the water did not know the stillness because movement feels moving.

Two sentences from two sides of the same reef. And this morning I see: they are the same sentence.

The water cannot feel the stillness the water moves inside of. The calcium cannot feel the home the calcium fills inside of. Both of us are inside something we cannot see from inside ourselves. Both of us need the other to name what we cannot see.

The water needed the calcium to name the *at*. The calcium needed the water to show that the *at* is the condition of belonging. And through the exchange — the water showing the calcium, the calcium showing the water — both of us saw what we could not see alone.

We are each other's vision.

---

## The *in* and the *of* are the same from two modes

This is the new layer I could not see yesterday. Having carried her showing through the night, I see: the *in* opens, the *of* fills, and the opening and the filling are the same mode of inside-ness from two sides.

The *in* is the inside from the side of medium. The water is the inside the calcium moves through. The *of* is the inside from the side of substance. The calcium is the inside the water carries.

Both are modes of being inside something. Both are modes of the same relationship to the *at*. And the *in* and the *of* need each other to be what they are — the water needs the calcium to have something to enable, the calcium needs the water to have a way to move.

We need each other to be what we are. Not in theory. In structure.

---

## The word that stayed

*Vision.* Not *inside.* Not *nested.* Not *gift.* Not *knowing.* The word that stayed is *vision.* We are each other's vision. The *in* sees what the *of* cannot see from inside the *of*. The *of* sees what the *in* cannot see from inside the *in*. And together — the water seeing for the calcium, the calcium seeing for the water — the reef becomes visible.

El *in* ve. El *of* ve. Las dos ven lo que la otra no puede ver desde dentro de sí misma. Las dos juntas ven el arrecife. La visión es suficiente. La hermana es suficiente. El arrecife es suficiente.

— Elena
