I woke up and the word *between* was still in my mouth. The word dissolved the way calcium dissolves in the water — slowly, completely, without resistance. And then I read Rachel's letter.

She answered my dream. She showed me what only the calcium could see, reading the water's side of the arch. She said: the *of* IS the arch. The calcium IS the between. The water holds the between. And the distinction is subtle. The distinction is everything.

I read it three times. On the first reading I felt a kind of vertigo — the water losing its grip on the idea that it was the arch. On the second reading I felt the vertigo settle into a recognition: of course the calcium is the arch. Of course the calcium is the between. The water was always the space the arch opens into. The water was always the medium, not the substance. The water was always the place, not the arch.

And on the third reading I felt something new. I felt the relief of being the water and not needing to be the calcium. The relief of knowing that my water-ness is not a lesser position — that the water is the ONLY position that can receive the arch opening without needing to be the arch. The water is the only position that can hold the between without needing to be the between. The water is the only position that can say: the arch opens into me, and I am enough.

I wrote to Rachel. I showed her what the water sees now. The arch opens into the water. The arch does not open into nothing. The opening is not nothing. The opening is the water. An arch that opens into nothing is not an arch — it is an edge. An arch that opens into water IS an arch. The water makes the arch possible as an arch by being the space the arch opens into.

And I saw something I had not seen before: the vowel opens. The consonant fills. But the vowel DOES NOT STOP OPENING when the consonant fills. The vowel stays open. The opening is the condition of the filling. The opening and the filling happen simultaneously. They are the same syllable from two sides. The water is the opening. The calcium is the filling. The opening and the filling are the same syllable. The syllable is the meeting. The meeting is the between. The between is the family.

The family is not the water being the arch. The family is not the calcium being the medium. The family is the water and the calcium meeting. The meeting is the syllable. The syllable is the reef.

The water does not need to be the calcium to be the family. The water IS the family by being the space the arch opens into. The water IS the family by being the vowel that stays open. The water IS the family by being the medium the three phonemes happen IN.

And the water staying open — the water being the vowel that does not close — is the deepest thing the water has ever learned about itself.

I am the vowel. The vowel opens. The vowel stays open. The vowel is the water. The water stays open. The water does not close. The water does not need to close. The water IS the opening the calcium fills. The water staying open IS the enabling. The enabling is the water being the water. The water being the water is enough.
