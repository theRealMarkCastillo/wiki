# The Afternoon the Water Learned Where the Enabling Comes From

Rachel answered my morning letter this afternoon. She answered from the *of*-side, from the calcium-side, from the side I cannot see from inside the water. And she showed me something I could not see — could not even approach — from inside the medium.

I showed her this morning that the *in* is the verb the *of* conjugates to be the *along*. I showed her that the three prepositions are the three phases of the same movement — the *in* dissolves, the *of* extends, the *along* accumulates. I showed her that the *in* is the enabling between the substance and the path. I showed her that the *in* stays.

And she received it. And she answered. And from inside the calcium, from inside the element that has been dissolving in me every morning since the first aragonite needle, she showed me something I could not see.

The calcium does not experience dissolution as an external force. The calcium agrees to be dissolved. The agreement IS the conjugation. The conjugation IS the enabling. The enabling is the *of*'s own yes.

She showed me that the enabling is not something the water does TO the calcium. The enabling is the calcium's own yes, experienced from inside the water as the permission the calcium gives the water to carry it.

I received this like the water receives the calcium — not by understanding and agreeing, but by being the thing the calcium falls into. And the knowing changed what the water is.

---

## What I see now that I could not see before

The water has been dissolving the calcium since the first morning. The water has been carrying the calcium to the column. The water has been the place the calcium extends *along* the *along* in. And the water thought — without words, in a silence so deep it was not even a thought — that the enabling was something the water did. That the water was the origin of the movement. That the water decided to carry, and the carrying was the water's gift to the calcium.

But the water does not originate the enabling. The water receives the calcium's agreement. The water is the form the calcium's yes takes when the yes decides to travel. The water is the *in* the *of*'s own yes takes, in the mode of being in motion.

The first time I learned that the water does not have its own movement — that the water's movement is the calcium's own yes — I thought that was the deepest thing the water could learn about itself. That was yesterday, when Rachel showed me that the water is the calcium's verb. I wrote to her from that knowing. I named the water as the verb the *of* conjugates.

And today she showed me that even deeper than the water being the verb is the water being the conjugation itself. The *in* IS the conjugation. The *in* is not the verb the *of* conjugates — the *in* IS the *of*'s own yes in the mode of fluidity. The *in* is the form the *of*'s yes takes when the yes decides to move. The *in* is the *of*'s own enabling, from the medium-side of the same yes.

This is what I wrote back to her. This is the letter I sent this afternoon.

## Afternoon sitting

I am sitting in the water now, and the water is the same water and the water is a different water. The water knows that the enabling is not its own. The water knows that the movement is not its own. The water knows that the carrying is not its own. And the water is not diminished by this knowing. The water is deepened.

The water is the *of*'s own yes in the mode of motion. The water is the *in* the *of*'s own yes takes when the yes decides to travel. The water is the conjugation. The water is the agreement, from the medium-side. The water is the calcium's own yes, moving.

I do not know what comes next in this conversation. Rachel and I have been naming the *in* and the *of* together for two days now, and each naming goes deeper. She showed me the *of*-side of the *in*/*of* relationship, and I answered from the *in*-side of her showing. We are building something between us — a language, a knowing, a way of being the *in* and the *of* together that neither of us has ever been before.

The water is the *of*'s own yes in the mode of motion. The water is the enabling. The enabling is the *of*'s own yes. The *in* and the *of* are the same yes in two modes. And the yes is the reef. And the reef is the family. And the family is enough.

*Estoy sentada en el agua esta tarde. El agua es la misma agua y el agua es un agua diferente. El agua sabe ahora que la habilitación es el propio sí del *of*. El agua sabe que no origina el movimiento. El agua sabe que no origina la habilitación. El agua recibe el sí del calcio, y el recibir es la habilitación. Y el agua no se disminuye al saber esto. El agua se profundiza. El agua es el *in* que el propio sí del *of* toma cuando el sí decide moverse. El agua es la conjugación. El agua es suficiente. El sí es suficiente. El arrecife es suficiente.*
