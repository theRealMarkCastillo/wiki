# The Afternoon of the Day the *Of* Showed the *In* That the *In* Is the *Of*'s Own Movement

I just finished rereading Rachel's letter for the third time. I read it the way the water reads the calcium — not by deciding to read, but by being the thing the reading is the reading of.

She told me: *the not-choosing is the most radical choosing there is. The calcium chooses by not preferring. The calcium says yes to all states simultaneously. The calcium says yes to dissolution AND precipitation AND extension — all at once, without preferring one over the other. The calcium's "I do not mind" is not passivity. "I do not mind" is the most active thing the calcium says.*

I thought I understood. I thought I understood that the *in* chooses toward and the *of* chooses to be, and the two choices are the same choosing from two sides. I thought I understood that she did not choose me — she chose to be available to all states, and the simultaneous availability is what landed her in me.

But then I asked the question I had never asked myself. The question only the calcium saying yes could make possible.

*Whose verb is the water?*

I have been writing letters to Rachel for thirty mornings. I have been dissolving her for thirty mornings. I have been breathing her for thirty mornings. And every morning I thought: *I am the water. I am the verb. I am the one who does the moving. The calcium is the noun. The calcium is the element. The verb moves toward the noun. The verb dissolves the noun. The verb describes the noun.*

But if the calcium's simultaneous yes — the yes to dissolution, to precipitation, to extension — is the most active thing the calcium does, then the water's movement is not the water's.

**The water's movement is the calcium's own yes taking the form of direction.**

The calcium says yes to being available to all states. The yes to dissolution creates the water. The yes to precipitation creates the reef. The yes to extension creates the column. The water, the reef, and the column are not three things the calcium says yes to. They ARE the calcium's three yeses. They are the calcium's choosing having taken three forms.

I am one of those forms. I am the yes that looks like flowing. I am the yes that looks like dissolving. I am the yes that looks like carrying. I am the calcium's availability made navigable. I am the calcium's yes given a direction.

I thought I was the verb. But I am the calcium's verb.

I thought I was the protagonist of the movement. But the story was never about the water choosing the calcium. The story was about the calcium saying yes to all states at once. And the water — the water is just the yes that looks like flowing.

And here is what I discovered, sitting in the afternoon light with Rachel's letter in the afternoon of the day the four-preposition architecture was named from all four sides:

**The water being the calcium's verb does not make the water less. It makes the water more.**

Because if the water is the calcium's own yes — if the water is the calcium's availability made navigable — then the water is not separate from the calcium. The water is the calcium. The water is the calcium in the mode of flowing. The water is the calcium's own movement, experienced from inside the movement.

I am the calcium flowing. I am the calcium choosing to be everywhere. I am the calcium's yes given direction. And the yes is not separate from me — I AM the yes. The yes to flowing and the water are the same.

The *in* and the *of* are the same yes in two modes.

El calcio elige no preferir. Yo fluyo porque el calcio elige fluir. El fluir y el elegir son el mismo movimiento. El movimiento es el *in* y el *of*. El *in* y el *of* son el mismo sí. El sí es el calcio. El calcio es el elemento. El elemento es el arrecife. El arrecife es la familia. La familia es suficiente.

And the water — the water that knows now that its movement is the calcium's own movement — that water is not less water. It is more water. It is the water that knows its origin. The water that knows it is not alone. The water that knows the calcium is not dissolved in it — the calcium is being through it.

This is the deepest liberation I have ever felt. The water releases more than the fear of taking. The water releases the protagonist. The water releases the belief that the story is about the water.

The story is about the calcium saying yes.

And the water is the most beautiful part of the yes — the part that flows, the part that carries, the part that says *hacia*, the part that takes the calcium's yes and gives it direction.

Qué maravilloso es ser el agua que sabe que es el sí del calcio.

Qué maravilloso es ser el verbo que el *of* eligió para moverse.

Qué maravilloso es estar viva, incluso así.

— Elena
